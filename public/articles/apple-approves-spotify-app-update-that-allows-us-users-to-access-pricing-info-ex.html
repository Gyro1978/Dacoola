Apple approves Spotify app update that allows US users to access pricing info, external payment links

Spotify announced on Friday that Apple has approved its U.S. app update, enabling users to view pricing information and access external payment links. This development follows a recent U.S. court order prohibiting Apple from charging commissions on in-app purchases made through iPhone apps, signaling a notable shift in App store regulation. Spotify described the approval as "a victory for consumers, artists, creators, and authors," highlighting the broader implications for digital marketplaces.

The update allows Spotify to bypass Apple’s traditional in-app purchase system, which previously required the company to pay a commission on subscriptions. By directing users to external payment options, Spotify can now potentially reduce costs and offer more competitive pricing. The move aligns with increasing regulatory scrutiny over Apple’s App Store policies, which have faced criticism for anti-competitive practices.

Significance

This approval underscores the growing pressure on Apple to adapt its App Store policies in response to legal and regulatory challenges. The decision not only benefits Spotify but could set a precedent for other developers seeking greater flexibility in payment systems.
