Google Gemini Updates to Take Center Stage at I/O 2025

Google I/O 2025, the company’s flagship developer conference, kicks off on May 20 at the Shoreline Amphitheatre in Mountain View. This year’s event will highlight significant Google Gemini updates, alongside Android 16 and other AI-driven innovations.

Key Announcements: Gemini and AI

Google’s Gemini AI models are set for major upgrades, with leaks pointing to an enhanced Gemini Ultra model. Gemini Ultra, the top-tier offering, may introduce new subscription plans-Premium Plus and Premium Pro-though pricing and features remain unclear. Currently, Gemini Advanced costs $20 per month for expanded chatbot capabilities.

Google will likely discuss Astra, its initiative for real-time, multimodal AI applications, and Project Mariner, which enables AI agents to perform web-based tasks autonomously. Code references in Google’s AI Studio hint at a "Computer Use" feature, possibly tied to Mariner.

Android 16 and Beyond

Ahead of I/O, Google will host The Android Show on May 13, focusing on Android 16. The update introduces Material 3 Expressive, a redesigned interface with dynamic elements, alongside Auracast support for seamless Bluetooth switching. Lock screen widgets and accessibility improvements are also expected.

Additional sessions will cover Chrome, Google Cloud, Wear OS, and Gemma, Google’s open AI models. Surprises like upgrades to NotebookLM or a "Video Overviews" tool leveraging Veo 2 could emerge.
