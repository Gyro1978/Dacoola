Microsoft Build Event 2025: AI Innovations, Keynote Details, and How to Watch

Microsoft Build, the tech giant’s premier developer conference, is set to take place from May 19 to 22 in Seattle. The event promises major AI announcements, hardware reveals, and insights into Microsoft’s latest innovations. With CEO Satya Nadella leading the keynote, attendees can expect updates on Copilot, Azure AI, and new developer tools.

For those unable to attend in person, Microsoft will livestream the keynote and breakout sessions for free, requiring only a simple registration. The event coincides with Google I/O, making it a pivotal week for tech enthusiasts.

Key Highlights of Microsoft Build 2025

The keynote, scheduled for May 19 from 12:05 p.m. to 1:30 p.m. ET, will likely focus on AI advancements, including updates to Office 365 integrations, Copilot+ PCs, and enterprise solutions. Last year’s event featured OpenAI collaborations, and similar high-profile partnerships could be announced this time.

Beyond the keynote, the session catalog offers deep dives into Azure AI, GitHub enhancements, and Fabric updates. Hardware launches are also anticipated, following last year’s Surface Laptop and Pro reveals.

How to Watch Microsoft Build 2025

Interested viewers can register for a free digital pass via Microsoft’s website, using LinkedIn, Microsoft, or GitHub credentials. The livestream will cover all major sessions, with ZDNET providing real-time coverage for those who miss the live event.

Frequently Asked Questions

When is the Microsoft Build 2025 keynote?

The keynote starts at 12:05 p.m. ET on May 19 and runs until 1:30 p.m. ET.

Is Microsoft Build 2025 free to attend online?

Yes, registration for the digital pass is free and grants access to livestreams.

What AI announcements are expected?

Updates to Copilot, Azure AI, and potential OpenAI collaborations are likely.
