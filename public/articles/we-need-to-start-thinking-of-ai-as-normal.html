We need to start thinking of AI as “normal”

Published on April 29, 2025 by AI News Team   |   View Original Source
<h2>AI Societal Impact: Why Experts Say AI Should Be Treated as "Normal"</h2> <p>Princeton researchers Arvind Narayanan and Sayash Kapoor challenge extreme views on AI societal impact, advocating for a "normal technology" framework. Their essay argues AI adoption will mirror historical tech like electricity—slow, uneven, and shaped by human oversight—not the rapid disruption often feared. </p> <p>The duo dismisses terms like "superintelligence" as speculative, emphasizing AI's role in amplifying existing societal issues rather than creating new ones. They critique the U.S.-China "AI arms race" narrative, calling for policies focused on democratic resilience and technical literacy instead of existential threats. </p> <p>Military AI applications remain a notable omission, with the authors citing classified data barriers but promising future analysis. Their approach reframes AI governance as a mundane yet critical task—aligning with slower, real-world adoption curves.</p>
