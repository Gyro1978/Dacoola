AI Societal Impact: Why Experts Say AI Should Be Treated as "Normal"

Princeton researchers Arvind Narayanan and Sayash Kapoor challenge extreme views on AI societal impact, advocating for a "normal technology" framework. Their essay argues AI adoption will mirror historical tech like electricity—slow, uneven, and shaped by human oversight—not the rapid disruption often feared.

The duo dismisses terms like "superintelligence" as speculative, emphasizing AI's role in amplifying existing societal issues rather than creating new ones. They critique the U.S.-China "AI arms race" narrative, calling for policies focused on democratic resilience and technical literacy instead of existential threats.

Military AI applications remain a notable omission, with the authors citing classified data barriers but promising future analysis. Their approach reframes AI governance as a mundane yet critical task—aligning with slower, real-world adoption curves.
