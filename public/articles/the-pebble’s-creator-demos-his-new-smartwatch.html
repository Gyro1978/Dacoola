The Pebble’s creator demos his new smartwatch

Eric Migicovsky, creator of the Pebble smartwatch, has debuted a podcast where he demonstrates the upcoming Core 2 Duo smartwatch from his new company, Core Devices. The device, set to ship in July 2025, runs on the open-source PebbleOS but is still in development, with basic features like timekeeping not yet enabled. Migicovsky also addressed concerns over US tariffs affecting pricing, as the watches are manufactured in Shenzhen. The Core 2 Duo features a black-and-white screen, a silicone strap, and a magnetic USB-C charger, while future models like the Core Time 2 will include a color touchscreen.

Why It Matters

Migicovsky’s return to smartwatches signals a revival of Pebble’s minimalist, functional design philosophy in an era dominated by feature-heavy wearables. The focus on open-source software and potential integration with Beeper—a universal chat app—could make these devices appealing to privacy-conscious users. Additionally, the EU’s Digital Markets Act may enable better iOS compatibility, expanding its market reach. However, ongoing tariff uncertainties highlight the challenges of hardware production in a globalized economy, which could impact pricing and adoption.
