Epic Games and Spotify Test Apple’s New App Store Policy

Epic Games and Spotify are pushing the boundaries of Apple’s revised App Store policies by submitting apps that would have been rejected under previous rules. On Friday, both companies sent updated versions of their apps for review, marking a significant test of Apple’s compliance with recent legal rulings.

For Epic Games, this could mean the return of Fortnite to the App Store after its removal in 2020. The game was banned after Epic introduced direct payment options, bypassing Apple’s in-app purchase system. Spotify, meanwhile, aims to let users buy audiobooks directly and offer premium subscribers additional listening hours beyond their monthly free allowance.

Key Changes and Legal Backdrop

Apple’s updated policies follow a federal judge’s ruling in the antitrust case filed by Epic Games. Though Apple largely won the lawsuit, the court ordered the company to relax restrictions on payment processing. Previously, developers had to request permission to link to external payment options and adhere to strict formatting rules, including warning screens discouraging off-platform purchases.

The new rules eliminate these barriers, allowing developers to direct users to web-based payment options without Apple’s interference. However, Apple initially attempted to impose a 27% commission on web sales, a move the court deemed non-compliant. The final policy removes this fee entirely, aligning with the judge’s intent to foster competition.

Market Implications

The shift could significantly impact developers, particularly smaller ones that rely on alternative payment models. While larger companies like Epic and Spotify stand to increase revenue, smaller developers may now have a better chance to compete without Apple’s steep commissions.

The approval of Amazon’s Kindle app, which now includes a direct purchase button, and Spotify’s recent update allowing U.S. users to see subscription pricing in-app, signal Apple’s gradual adaptation to the new rules. More apps are expected to follow suit, testing the limits of the revised policy.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why was *Fortnite* removed from the App Store?

Apple removed *Fortnite* in 2020 after Epic Games introduced a direct payment system, violating App Store rules. This led to a legal battle over Apple’s antitrust practices.

What changes did the court mandate for Apple?

The court ruled that Apple must allow developers to link to external payment options without restrictions or commissions, promoting fair competition.

How does this affect Spotify’s audiobook sales?

Spotify can now offer direct audiobook purchases and additional listening hours without Apple’s fees, potentially increasing revenue and user flexibility.
