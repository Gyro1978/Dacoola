Gemini on Wear OS: Google Expands AI Assistant to Smartwatches and Google TV

Google has officially announced that its AI assistant, Gemini, is coming to Wear OS smartwatches and Google TV, marking a significant expansion of its AI ecosystem. The move, revealed during the Android Show ahead of Google I/O, aligns with the company’s broader strategy to replace Google Assistant with Gemini across all devices. Users can soon interact with Gemini naturally on their wrists or through their TVs, enabling hands-free assistance in scenarios where phones aren’t practical-like during workouts or while cooking.

Key Features and Use Cases

Gemini on Wear OS will allow users to set reminders, retrieve information, and interact with apps without needing their phones. For example, a runner can ask, "What’s the nearest charging station?" or a parent can request "kid-friendly action movies" on Google TV. The AI’s contextual understanding means users don’t need precise phrasing-a major upgrade over traditional voice assistants.

Google also confirmed Gemini’s integration with Android XR for extended reality devices, like the upcoming Samsung Project Moohan headset. This will enable immersive planning, such as vacation itineraries with maps and videos, all powered by Gemini’s AI.

Expanding Beyond Phones

The shift to Wear OS and Google TV underscores Google’s focus on accessibility. "Gemini can help you create an entire itinerary in minutes within this rich, immersive experience," said Guemmy Kim, Google’s senior director of product. Later this year, Android Auto will also leverage Gemini for tasks like summarizing messages or locating charging stations mid-drive.

Pros & Cons

  • **Natural language processing** reduces the need for exact commands.
Cons

  • **Limited rollout timeline**-Wear OS support arrives in "coming months."

Frequently Asked Questions

When will Gemini launch on Wear OS?

Google confirmed a release in the "coming months," but no exact date was provided.

Will Gemini replace Google Assistant entirely?

Yes, Google plans to phase out Google Assistant in favor of Gemini across all platforms.

What devices support Gemini besides Wear OS?

Gemini is coming to Google TV, Android Auto, and Android XR devices like the Samsung Project Moohan headset.
