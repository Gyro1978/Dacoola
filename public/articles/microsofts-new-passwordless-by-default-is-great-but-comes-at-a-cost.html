Microsoft’s new “passwordless by default” is great but comes at a cost

Published on May 02, 2025 by AI News Team   |   View Original Source
<h2>Microsoft’s new “passwordless by default” is great but comes at a cost</h2> <p>Microsoft is taking a major step toward eliminating passwords by making <strong>passwordless authentication</strong> the default option for new user accounts. The company announced this initiative as part of an industry-wide effort to move away from traditional passwords, which have long been a weak point in cybersecurity. By prioritizing passkeys—a more secure alternative—Microsoft aims to reduce the risks of phishing and credential theft. </p> <p>A key component of this shift is the adoption of passkeys, a technology developed in collaboration with Google, Apple, and the FIDO Alliance. Passkeys provide a more secure and user-friendly login method, eliminating the need for memorizing complex passwords. Microsoft will now prompt existing users to set up a passkey upon their next login, while new users will automatically default to this method. </p> <p>This move aligns with broader industry trends toward stronger authentication standards. While the transition may require adjustments for some users, the long-term benefits—such as reduced security breaches and simplified logins—could outweigh the initial challenges.</p>
