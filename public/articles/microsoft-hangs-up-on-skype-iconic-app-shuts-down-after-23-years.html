Microsoft Hangs Up on Skype: Iconic App Shuts Down After 23 Years

Microsoft is officially shutting down Skype, the pioneering video-calling service, after 23 years. The company is encouraging users to transition to Microsoft Teams, which offers similar communication features. The Skype shutdown marks the end of an era for one of the earliest and most recognizable VoIP platforms.

Why It Matters

Skype revolutionized digital communication by making video calls accessible to millions, paving the way for modern platforms like Zoom and FaceTime. Its shutdown reflects Microsoft’s strategic shift toward Teams, which integrates better with enterprise workflows. While longtime users may feel nostalgic, the move underscores the tech industry’s rapid evolution—older services often give way to more advanced, integrated solutions.
