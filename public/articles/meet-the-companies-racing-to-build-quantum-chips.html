Meet the companies racing to build quantum chips

Quantum computing has long been touted as "just around the corner," but several companies are now working to turn this promise into a commercial reality. These quantum computing chips aim to solve complex problems beyond the reach of classical computers, spanning fields like medicine, cybersecurity, materials science, and chemistry. However, significant practical challenges remain before widespread adoption.

Why It Matters

The race to develop viable quantum computing chips could revolutionize industries by enabling breakthroughs in drug discovery, encryption, and material design. While still in its early stages, the progress signals a shift from theoretical research to real-world applications. If successful, these advancements could redefine computational limits, though scalability and error correction remain key hurdles.
