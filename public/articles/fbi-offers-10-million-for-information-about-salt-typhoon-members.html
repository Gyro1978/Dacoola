FBI Offers $10 Million for Information About Salt Typhoon Members

The FBI has announced a $10 million reward for information regarding members of Salt Typhoon, a China-state hacking group linked to intrusions into sensitive networks of multiple US telecommunications companies. The agency is also offering relocation assistance and additional compensation to individuals who provide actionable intelligence about the group’s activities. Salt Typhoon is among several hacking groups operating on behalf of the People’s Republic of China, known for conducting cyber espionage campaigns.

According to intelligence agencies and private security firms, Salt Typhoon has been involved in a series of attacks aimed at gathering critical information, potentially for use in future military conflicts. The FBI’s recent statement specifically seeks details about the group’s members and their compromise of US telecom networks in 2024. The agency has published a public service announcement urging anyone with relevant information to come forward.

A Broad and Significant Cyber Campaign

The FBI’s $10 million reward underscores the severity of Salt Typhoon’s cyber operations, which have targeted vital US infrastructure. The group’s activities align with broader Chinese state-sponsored hacking efforts, raising concerns about national security risks. The reward program highlights the US government’s intensified focus on countering foreign cyber threats.
