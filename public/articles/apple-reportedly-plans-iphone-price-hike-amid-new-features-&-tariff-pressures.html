Apple Reportedly Plans iPhone Price Hike Amid New Features & Tariff Pressures

Apple is reportedly preparing to increase prices for its upcoming iPhone lineup set to launch this autumn, according to The Wall Street Journal. While the company aims to avoid directly linking the price hikes to U.S. tariffs on Chinese imports, sources suggest Apple may justify the increases with new features and design changes. The tariffs are expected to cost Apple $900 million in Q3 2025, though the company has not confirmed their direct impact on pricing.

To mitigate tariff costs, Apple has shifted more iPhone production to India. Meanwhile, the U.S. and China have temporarily paused reciprocal tariffs for 90 days, with exemptions for smartphones and other tech products. Despite this, a 20% tariff on Chinese goods remains in effect.

Key Factors Behind the iPhone Price Increase

Apple’s strategy appears to focus on framing the price hike around innovation rather than tariffs. Reports indicate the upcoming iPhone models will include an ultra-thin design, potentially justifying higher costs. Additionally, Apple is reportedly planning a radical redesign for 2027-a "mostly glass, curved iPhone" without any cutouts-to mark the iPhone’s 20th anniversary.

The company’s supply chain adjustments, including increased manufacturing in India, highlight efforts to reduce reliance on China. However, analysts suggest that tariffs and rising component costs could still influence final pricing.
