A Google Gemini model now has a “dial” to adjust how much it reasons

Published on April 17, 2025 by AI News Team   |   View Original Source
<h2>Google Gemini Model Now Features Adjustable AI Reasoning Control</h2> <p>Google DeepMind has added a "reasoning dial" to its top Gemini AI model, allowing developers to control how much the system "thinks" before responding. This AI reasoning control feature aims to reduce unnecessary computational costs and energy use, addressing concerns that models often overthink simple queries. The update targets developers building apps, letting them set budgets for computing power per task. </p> <h3>Balancing Cost and Performance</h3> <p>Reasoning models, which logically work through problems, have surged in popularity but can be expensive—some tasks cost over $200 to complete. While beneficial for complex tasks like coding or research, they often waste resources on simpler prompts. Google’s new slider lets developers scale back reasoning intensity where it’s not needed, cutting costs by up to six times. </p> <p>The move reflects a shift in AI development, where longer "thinking" times replace sheer model size as a performance booster. However, experts warn that overuse of reasoning models can lead to inefficiencies, like repetitive loops or excessive energy consumption.</p>
