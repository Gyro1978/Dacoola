Trump Pressures Apple to Move iPhone Manufacturing From India to US

President Donald Trump has publicly urged Apple CEO Tim Cook to halt iPhone manufacturing expansion in India and prioritize production in the US instead. Speaking at a business summit in Doha, Trump emphasized his dissatisfaction with Apple's growing operations in India, citing high tariffs. The comments follow Apple's recent announcement of a $500 billion US expansion plan and India's approval of a $435 million Foxconn project for Apple chip production.

Political and Economic Tensions Over iPhone Production

Trump's remarks highlight ongoing tensions between the US and India over trade policies. He specifically criticized India as "one of the highest tariff nations in the world," urging Apple to redirect investments stateside. While Trump claimed Apple would increase US production, no concrete timeline or details were provided.

Apple has been steadily expanding its manufacturing footprint in India, with reports indicating that 20% of iPhones are already produced there. The company aims to import all US-bound iPhones from India by 2026, reducing reliance on China. However, Trump's intervention could complicate these plans.

Apple’s Balancing Act Between India and the US

Apple's $500 billion US expansion includes new facilities, hiring over 20,000 employees, and establishing a manufacturing academy. Yet, its parallel investments in India-such as Foxconn’s chip production project-signal a strategic diversification. The conflicting pressures from Trump and India’s growing manufacturing appeal put Apple in a challenging position.
