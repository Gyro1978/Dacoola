The End of an AI That Shocked the World: OpenAI Retires GPT-4

OpenAI has announced the GPT-4 retirement, marking the end of one of the most influential AI models in history. Effective April 30, 2025, GPT-4 will be fully replaced by GPT-4o in ChatGPT, closing a chapter on the model that accelerated the global AI race when it launched in March 2023. While the public-facing version of GPT-4 will no longer be available in ChatGPT, OpenAI confirmed that the model will remain accessible via its API, ensuring developers can still utilize it for their projects.

GPT-4’s retirement follows its groundbreaking debut, where it demonstrated capabilities that stunned observers—such as scoring in the 90th percentile on the Uniform Bar Exam and excelling in AP tests. Its release triggered both immense hype and existential concerns about AI’s ability to mimic human communication and reasoning. The model’s performance set a new benchmark for generative AI, fueling debates about regulation and ethical implications.

Significance

The transition from GPT-4 to GPT-4o signifies OpenAI’s commitment to advancing AI technology while phasing out older models. GPT-4’s legacy lies in its role as a catalyst for the AI boom, shaping industry standards and public discourse around artificial intelligence.
