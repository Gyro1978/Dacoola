Greece Tech Economy: The Mediterranean’s Rising Innovation Powerhouse

Greece, long celebrated for its history and tourism, is quietly emerging as a formidable player in the global tech economy. Prime Minister Kyriakos Mitsotakis revealed in an exclusive interview with TechCrunch that the country is leveraging AI, policy reforms, and a growing startup ecosystem to redefine its economic future. With initiatives like tax incentives for returning talent and a focus on digital government services, Greece is positioning itself as Europe’s next innovation hotspot.

From Crisis to Tech-Driven Revival

A decade after its financial collapse, Greece is rewriting its economic narrative. Mitsotakis, a former venture capitalist, outlined how the country made a "conscious decision" to diversify beyond tourism. "Tech could be 10% of our economy," he said, citing rising startup activity and foreign investment. Greece’s late entry into Europe’s tech scene has proven advantageous-avoiding the inflated valuations plaguing mature markets while embracing AI to "leapfrog" competitors.

Key to this transformation is digitization. Greece has outpaced Germany in modernizing public services, with AI slashing contract review times from hours to minutes. The government is also a proactive client for startups, particularly in health and defense, fostering a "startup mentality" within its own institutions.

Talent and Investment: Fueling the Ecosystem

Reversing the brain drain remains critical. Greece offers a 50% income tax break for seven years to lure skilled workers, but Mitsotakis acknowledges that opportunity matters more than incentives. New "Golden Visa" rules grant residency to foreign investors funding Greek startups, while programs target global entrepreneurs. "This is a war for talent," he emphasized, noting regional hubs like Thessaloniki and Heraklion are vital for balanced growth.

Challenges persist, including slow legal reforms and limited late-stage capital. Yet, Greece’s stability and momentum are undeniable. "We’ve broken out of the vicious cycle," Mitsotakis said, framing tech as a catalyst for lasting change.
