Smart Shirt Tracks Workouts—and Goes Straight to the Wash

Researchers at Cornell University have created a smart workout shirt called SeamFit, which tracks exercise and posture with high accuracy while maintaining comfort and washability. Unlike bulky wearables, SeamFit uses conductive threads sewn into the shirt’s seams to detect body movements via capacitance changes. A detachable circuit board records data and sends it via Bluetooth to an AI system, achieving 93.4% exercise classification accuracy. The shirt works even after multiple washes, making it practical for fitness and medical applications.

Why It Matters

This innovation addresses key challenges in smart clothing—comfort, durability, and ease of use—while delivering reliable fitness tracking. Its washable design and high accuracy could make it appealing not just to athletes but also for physical therapy and rehabilitation. Additionally, the technology could aid robotics by capturing human motion data for safer human-robot interactions. If commercialized, SeamFit could push smart clothing closer to mainstream adoption, blending seamlessly into everyday life.
