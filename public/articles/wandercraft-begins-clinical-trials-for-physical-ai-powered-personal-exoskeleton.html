Wandercraft Begins Clinical Trials for Physical AI-Powered Personal Exoskeleton

Wandercraft, a robotics startup founded by Nicolas Simon, has initiated clinical trials for its AI-powered exoskeleton, designed to help individuals with mobility challenges stand and walk independently. Simon, whose family members have Charcot-Marie-Tooth disease, developed the technology to assist wheelchair users, including an estimated 80 million people worldwide. The Personal Exoskeleton uses AI-driven mechanisms for stability and movement, controlled via a joystick, and aims to enable users to navigate daily life more freely.

The company’s first exoskeleton, Atalante X, is already FDA-cleared and used in over 100 rehabilitation centers globally. The newer Personal Exoskeleton, now in trials in New York and New Jersey, leverages physical AI to adapt in real time to different surfaces like concrete and carpet. Wandercraft is utilizing NVIDIA Isaac Sim and Omniverse to refine reinforcement learning and accelerate development. The team also explores NVIDIA Jetson Thor for edge computing, enhancing the device’s responsiveness.

Beyond mobility, the exoskeleton could mitigate health risks associated with prolonged sitting, such as cardiovascular issues. Wandercraft demonstrated the prototype at NVIDIA GTC with Caroline Laubach, a spinal stroke survivor, and plans to seek FDA clearance post-trial. With potential Medicare coverage, the device may soon help millions in the U.S. regain independence.
