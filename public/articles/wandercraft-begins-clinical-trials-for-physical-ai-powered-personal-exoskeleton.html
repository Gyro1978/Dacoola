Wandercraft Begins Clinical Trials for Physical AI-Powered Personal Exoskeleton

Wandercraft, a robotics startup founded by Nicolas Simon, has launched clinical trials for its AI-powered exoskeleton, designed to help individuals with mobility challenges regain independence. Simon, whose family members live with Charcot-Marie-Tooth disease, developed the technology to assist wheelchair users, including an estimated 80 million people worldwide who rely on mobility aids. The Personal Exoskeleton enables users to stand and walk with AI-driven stability, controlled via a joystick.

The company’s first exoskeleton, Atalante X, is already FDA-cleared and used in over 100 rehabilitation centers globally, helping patients take more than one million steps per month. The new Personal Exoskeleton, currently in trials in New York and New Jersey, integrates AI to adapt in real time to different surfaces like concrete and carpet. Wandercraft leverages NVIDIA’s simulation tools, including Isaac Sim and Jetson Thor, to refine its reinforcement learning models and enhance exoskeleton responsiveness.

Advancing Development With Physical AI

Wandercraft’s AI-driven approach aims to make daily mobility seamless for users, reducing health risks associated with prolonged wheelchair use. The company showcased the prototype at NVIDIA GTC, where spinal stroke survivor Caroline Laubach demonstrated its capabilities. With plans to seek FDA clearance post-trial, Wandercraft hopes to make the exoskeleton accessible to millions, potentially covered by Medicare. Simon envisions the technology empowering users to navigate urban environments effortlessly, marking a significant step toward inclusive mobility solutions.
