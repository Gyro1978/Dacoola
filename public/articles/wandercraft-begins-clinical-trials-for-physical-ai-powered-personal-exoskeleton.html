Wandercraft Begins Clinical Trials for Physical AI-Powered Personal Exoskeleton

Wandercraft, a robotics startup founded by Nicolas Simon, has launched clinical trials for its AI-powered exoskeleton, designed to help individuals with mobility impairments walk again. Simon, whose family members have Charcot-Marie-Tooth disease, was inspired to create a device that could restore movement for wheelchair users. The Personal Exoskeleton, controlled via a joystick, uses AI to provide stability and real-time movement adaptation, potentially benefiting the estimated 80 million people worldwide who rely on wheelchairs.

The company’s first exoskeleton, Atalante X, is already FDA-cleared and used in over 100 rehabilitation centers globally. The new Personal Exoskeleton, currently in trials in New York and New Jersey, is designed for daily indoor and outdoor use. It leverages NVIDIA’s AI infrastructure, including Isaac Sim for reinforcement learning, to improve responsiveness and adaptability across different surfaces like concrete and carpet. Simon emphasized the importance of speed and real-world usability, stating that the goal is to enable users to walk at an average pace and navigate stairs.

Advancing Mobility with AI and Robotics

Wandercraft’s exoskeleton could significantly reduce health risks associated with prolonged sitting, such as cardiovascular and digestive issues. The company is also exploring NVIDIA Isaac for Healthcare and Jetson Thor to enhance its AI-driven robotics development. A prototype was recently showcased at NVIDIA GTC with Caroline Laubach, a spinal stroke survivor, demonstrating its potential. With clinical trials expected to conclude this year, Wandercraft aims to seek FDA clearance and expand accessibility, supported by anticipated Medicare coverage.
