Nvidia Endorses Trump’s Plan to Rescind AI Chip Restrictions

The Trump administration is expected to roll back Biden-era restrictions on AI chip sales, a move welcomed by Nvidia. According to Bloomberg, the policy shift aims to revise semiconductor export controls, potentially lifting curbs on companies like Nvidia. The chipmaker praised the decision, framing it as a chance to boost U.S. leadership in AI and create domestic jobs.

Biden’s restrictions, likened to past limits on PlayStation 2 exports over supercomputer concerns, faced pushback from tech firms and foreign governments. While the repeal isn’t finalized, Trump’s team reportedly won’t enforce the "AI Diffusion Rule" set to take effect May 15. Nvidia’s stock rose following the news, coinciding with CEO Jensen Huang’s earlier White House visit.

Policy Shift and Industry Impact

The proposed reversal aligns with Trump’s broader efforts to ease trade barriers, particularly as Middle Eastern allies like Saudi Arabia and the UAE resisted the chip limits. Nvidia’s statement emphasized the economic upside, suggesting the change could reduce trade deficits and strengthen U.S. infrastructure. Analysts note the restrictions had strained relationships with key markets, making the pivot a strategic win for chip exporters.

Pros & Cons
