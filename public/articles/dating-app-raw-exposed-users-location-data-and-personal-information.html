Dating app Raw exposed users’ location data and personal information

The dating app Raw, which markets itself as a secure platform with end-to-end encryption, has suffered a significant data breach, exposing users' personal information and granular location data. This dating app data breach reportedly made sensitive details, including dating preferences and real-time location data, accessible on the open web. The incident raises serious concerns about the app's security measures, despite its privacy assurances.

According to reports, Raw's security failure allowed unauthorized access to highly personal user data, potentially putting individuals at risk. The exposed information could be exploited for stalking, harassment, or other malicious activities. The breach contradicts the app's claims of robust encryption, highlighting a gap between its advertised security and actual practices.

Significance

This incident underscores the risks associated with dating apps handling sensitive user data. A breach of this nature not only compromises privacy but also erodes trust in platforms that promise secure interactions. Users are advised to review their privacy settings and consider the implications of sharing location data on such apps.
