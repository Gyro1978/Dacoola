Meta Begins Testing Threads Video Ads in Latest Platform Update

Instagram Threads will soon feature video ads, Meta confirmed on Thursday. The test, which mirrors competitor X’s approach, marks an expansion of Threads’ advertising capabilities after opening ads to global advertisers last month. The announcement was made during Meta’s presentation at the IAB NewFronts, where social media platforms showcase their offerings to advertisers.

Key Features and Market Impact

A select group of advertisers will test 19:9 or 1:1 video ads, which will appear in the Threads feed alongside organic content. Meta has not disclosed pricing or ad frequency details yet. This move follows Threads’ recent milestone of surpassing 350 million monthly active users, along with a 35% increase in user engagement due to improved recommendation systems, as shared by CEO Mark Zuckerberg in April.

Meta also introduced Reels trending ads, a short-form video solution that places ads next to popular creator content. This aligns with TikTok’s Pulse Suite, which recently expanded to let advertisers target trending topics across categories like sports and entertainment.

Expanding Advertiser Tools

Beyond Threads, Meta is testing Trends in Instagram’s Creator Marketplace to help brands identify popular content themes. The company is also rolling out Video Expansion on Facebook Reels, which adjusts video aspect ratios for a more seamless viewing experience.

Meta is currently testing video ads with a small group of advertisers, but a full rollout date has not been announced.

How will Threads video ads differ from X’s ads?

Threads will use 19:9 or 1:1 video formats, similar to Instagram Reels, while X’s approach varies by placement.

What’s driving Threads’ growth?

Improved recommendation systems have increased user engagement by 35%, contributing to its 350 million monthly active users.
