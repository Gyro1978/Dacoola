Giant Radar ‘Umbrella’ Launches Into Space on Mission to Transform Climate Science

The European Space Agency (ESA) successfully launched its Biomass satellite aboard a Vega-C rocket from Kourou, French Guiana. The satellite features a massive radar antenna, resembling a space umbrella, designed by US defense contractor L3 Harris. This first-of-its-kind space radar satellite will take nine days to fully deploy its intricate antenna, which will direct P-band radar pulses to Earth’s forests, gathering critical climate data.

Why It Matters

This mission marks a significant advancement in climate science, as the Biomass satellite will provide unprecedented insights into global forest biomass—a key factor in carbon storage and climate change. The P-band radar’s ability to penetrate dense foliage allows for more accurate forest monitoring than ever before. The data collected could refine climate models and inform global conservation efforts, reinforcing ESA’s role in pioneering Earth observation technologies.
