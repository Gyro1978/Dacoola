NVIDIA Brings Cybersecurity to Every AI Factory

As enterprises increasingly adopt AI, securing AI factories—where complex, agentic workflows are executed—has become a critical priority. NVIDIA addresses this challenge with its new AI cybersecurity framework, DOCA Argus, part of the NVIDIA cybersecurity AI platform. Running on the NVIDIA BlueField networking platform, DOCA Argus provides runtime threat detection on every node, ensuring immediate response to attacks while integrating seamlessly with enterprise security systems.

Unlike traditional security tools, DOCA Argus operates independently of the host, requiring no agents or host-based resources. This agentless design enables detection speeds up to 1,000x faster than conventional solutions, without impacting system performance. By functioning outside the host, Argus remains invisible to attackers, even in compromised environments, making it ideal for containerized and multi-tenant infrastructures.

The framework leverages advanced memory forensics to monitor threats in real time, reducing false positives and delivering actionable intelligence. Enterprises can integrate DOCA Argus with SIEM, SOAR, and XDR platforms, enabling continuous monitoring and automated threat mitigation. NVIDIA’s collaboration with Cisco further strengthens AI factory security by embedding protection at every infrastructure layer, ensuring runtime security from deployment.

Significance

With the rise of agentic AI and large-scale AI workloads, NVIDIA’s DOCA Argus provides enterprises with a resilient, high-performance security solution. By combining BlueField’s acceleration with Argus’ proactive detection, businesses can scale AI adoption confidently while safeguarding critical data.
