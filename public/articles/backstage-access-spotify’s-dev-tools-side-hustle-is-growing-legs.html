Backstage access: Spotify’s dev tools side-hustle is growing legs

Published on May 04, 2025 by AI News Team   |   View Original Source
<h2>Backstage access: Spotify’s dev tools side-hustle is growing legs</h2> <p>Spotify, primarily known for its music-streaming service, has been quietly expanding its developer tooling business through Backstage, an open-source platform launched in 2020. The tool has now been adopted by over 2 million developers across 3,400 organizations, including major companies like Airbnb, LinkedIn, and Twilio. While ads and subscriptions remain Spotify’s core revenue drivers, Backstage’s rapid adoption highlights the company’s diversification into developer infrastructure. </p> <h3>Why It Matters</h3> <p>Spotify’s Backstage success underscores the growing demand for unified developer portals that streamline software development workflows. By open-sourcing the tool, Spotify has positioned itself as a key player in the DevOps ecosystem, fostering collaboration beyond its traditional market. This expansion could pave the way for monetization opportunities, such as enterprise support or premium features, further diversifying Spotify’s revenue streams in a competitive tech landscape.</p>
