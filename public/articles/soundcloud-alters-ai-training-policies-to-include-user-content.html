SoundCloud Alters AI Training Policies to Include User Content

SoundCloud has updated its terms of use to permit AI training on audio uploaded by users, according to a recent policy change spotted by tech ethicist Ed-Newton Rex. The revised terms, effective February 7, grant the platform rights to use uploaded content to "inform, train, develop, or serve as input" for AI technologies. While third-party agreements with record labels are exempt, no explicit opt-out option is currently available for individual creators.

Policy Shift and Industry Trends

The move aligns SoundCloud with other platforms like X, LinkedIn, and YouTube, which have similarly adjusted their AI training policies in recent months. SoundCloud's updated terms emphasize that user content may be used for AI development as part of its services. The platform has existing partnerships with major music publishers, including Universal Music and Warner Music Group, though independent creators lack clear opt-out mechanisms.

Last year, SoundCloud collaborated with AI vendors to introduce tools for remixing and vocal generation, pledging ethical AI practices. However, the lack of transparency around this latest change has raised concerns among users who argue for opt-in consent and fair compensation for contributions to AI datasets.

Pros & Cons

Pros
  • Enables *faster* AI development for music-related tools, benefiting creators with new features.
  • Aligns with industry trends, potentially improving platform competitiveness.
Cons
  • Lack of opt-out options may alienate independent creators concerned about unauthorized use.
  • Raises ethical questions about compensation and consent for training data.

Frequently Asked Questions

Currently, SoundCloud does not provide an explicit opt-out setting for individual creators, though third-party agreements may override the policy.

X, LinkedIn, and YouTube have also updated terms to allow AI training on user data in recent months.
