Skype Shuts Down After 23 Years

After more than two decades, Skype is shutting down as Microsoft integrates the once-dominant video calling platform into Microsoft Teams. Unable to keep up with rivals like Zoom and FaceTime, the iconic service will be discontinued, marking the end of an era in digital communication.

Why It Matters

Skype’s shutdown highlights the rapid evolution of communication tools and the challenges legacy platforms face in staying relevant. Microsoft’s decision to fold Skype into Teams reflects a broader industry shift toward unified collaboration platforms. For users, this transition signals the need to adapt to newer, more competitive alternatives—underscoring how quickly tech landscapes can change.
