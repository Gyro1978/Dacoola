Apple Explores Adding AI Search Engines to Safari

Published on May 07, 2025 by AI News Team   |   View Original Source
<h2>Apple Explores Adding AI Search Engines to Safari</h2> <p>Apple is reportedly in talks to integrate <strong>AI search engines</strong> from OpenAI, Perplexity, and Anthropic into Safari, according to Bloomberg. The revelation came during Eddy Cue, Apple’s Senior Vice President of Services, testimony in the U.S. Justice Department’s lawsuit against Alphabet. Cue highlighted that Safari searches declined for the first time last month, attributing the shift to the growing adoption of AI-powered alternatives. </p> <p>While Apple’s $20 billion-a-year deal with Google remains intact, Cue suggested that <strong>AI search providers</strong> could eventually replace traditional search engines. However, he noted these services may not become Safari’s default option immediately, as they still require refinement. </p> <h3>The Shift Toward AI-Powered Search</h3> <p>Apple’s exploration of AI-driven search engines marks a pivotal moment in the tech industry.
