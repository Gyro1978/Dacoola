Netflix Introduces AI-Powered Search Tool for Smarter Content Discovery

Netflix has officially unveiled its new AI-powered search tool, leveraging OpenAI’s ChatGPT to transform how users discover content. Announced at its recent tech and product event, the feature allows subscribers to search using natural language queries like “I want something funny and upbeat” or “scary but not too scary.” The tool is rolling out this week as an opt-in beta for iOS users, with initial testing already underway in Australia and New Zealand.

This move places Netflix alongside competitors like Amazon and Tubi, which have experimented with AI-driven search. However, Tubi discontinued its similar feature due to low adoption—raising questions about Netflix’s long-term strategy. Beyond search, Netflix also revealed plans to use generative AI for localized title cards and introduced a short-form video feed for mobile users.

How Netflix’s AI-Powered Search Works

The new tool integrates ChatGPT to interpret conversational queries, offering tailored recommendations based on mood, genre, or even nuanced preferences. Unlike traditional keyword-based searches, this approach mimics human dialogue, making content discovery more intuitive. For example, a request like “a thriller with a strong female lead” would generate curated results.

Early tests suggest the feature excels at handling vague or multi-layered requests, a significant upgrade over rigid search algorithms. However, its success hinges on user engagement—a challenge Tubi’s discontinued experiment highlights.

Pros & Cons

Pros

Frequently Asked Questions

When will the AI search tool be available globally?

Netflix plans a phased rollout, starting with iOS users in Australia and New Zealand. Wider availability depends on beta feedback.

Does Netflix’s AI search store my queries?

Netflix states queries are anonymized, but privacy policies may evolve as the feature scales.
