Tesla Robotaxi Investigation: NHTSA Demands Answers on FSD Safety

Federal safety investigators are intensifying scrutiny of Tesla’s upcoming robotaxi service, demanding detailed explanations about how its Full Self-Driving (Supervised) software performs in low-visibility conditions. The National Highway Traffic Safety Administration (NHTSA) sent Tesla a formal inquiry this week, seeking clarity on the company’s safety evaluation methods ahead of its planned June launch in Austin, Texas.

The probe follows Tesla’s April 23 announcement that its FSD Supervised ride-hailing service had completed over 1,500 trips in Austin and San Francisco. However, NHTSA’s letter highlights lingering concerns about the system’s ability to handle challenges like sun glare, fog, and dust-issues linked to four prior crashes under investigation since October.

NHTSA’s Key Concerns and Tesla’s Response

The agency’s letter, first reported by Reuters, presses Tesla for specifics on how its robotaxi fleet-reportedly using the same FSD software-will ensure safety in adverse conditions. Investigators asked for details on fleet size, vehicle models, and whether the autonomous system differs from the current FSD Supervised product, which requires driver oversight.

Tesla CEO Elon Musk has previously stated that robotaxis will eventually operate without supervision, but NHTSA’s inquiry underscores regulatory skepticism. The agency also wants Tesla to outline its validation process for public road use, particularly for edge cases like rain or snow.

Low-Visibility Risks and Regulatory Hurdles

NHTSA’s focus on environmental challenges reflects broader industry struggles with autonomous vehicle reliability. Tesla’s FSD, while advanced, remains classified as a Level 2 system, meaning drivers must stay alert. The robotaxi ambition hinges on achieving full autonomy-a leap regulators are wary of without robust safety data.

Tesla has until late May to respond. The outcome could delay or reshape its robotaxi rollout, a key pillar of Musk’s vision for the company.
