The Future of AI Processing

AI hardware allocation is shifting as more processing moves to inference and the edge, reducing reliance on cloud computing. Advances in chip technology and heterogeneous compute enable dynamic workload distribution across CPUs, GPUs, and NPUs, optimizing performance and energy efficiency.

Edge and Heterogeneous Compute Take Center Stage

Edge processing enhances response times and privacy by running AI models on devices like smartphones and industrial IoT systems. Meanwhile, heterogeneous compute allows organizations to balance latency, security, and power usage by assigning workloads to the best-suited hardware.
