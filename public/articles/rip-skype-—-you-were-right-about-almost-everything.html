RIP Skype — you were right about almost everything

Microsoft officially shut down Skype on May 2025, nearly 14 years after acquiring it for $8.5 billion. The once-revolutionary platform, which FCC chairman Michael Powell in 2003 hailed as the future of communication, ultimately saw its relevance fade as competitors like Zoom, Teams, and WhatsApp took over. Despite its decline, Skype’s early vision of free, high-quality global calls reshaped the telecom industry—only to be left behind by the very trends it pioneered.

Why It Matters

Skype’s shutdown underscores how quickly tech landscapes evolve. It was among the first to prove that internet-based communication could rival traditional telecom, paving the way for today’s dominant apps. Yet, its inability to adapt—while Microsoft shifted focus to Teams—highlights the risks of resting on early success. This Skype retrospective serves as a reminder that even groundbreaking innovations must continuously evolve to stay relevant.
