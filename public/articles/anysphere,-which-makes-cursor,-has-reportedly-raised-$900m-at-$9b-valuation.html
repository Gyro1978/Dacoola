Anysphere, which makes Cursor, has reportedly raised $900M at $9B valuation

Anysphere, the company behind the AI-powered coding tool Cursor, has reportedly raised $900 million in a new funding round led by Thrive Capital, according to the Financial Times. The round, which also includes participation from Andreessen Horowitz (a16z) and Accel, values Anysphere at approximately $9 billion. This significant AI coding tool funding round highlights growing investor confidence in AI-driven development tools.

Why It Matters

This massive funding round underscores the increasing demand for AI-powered coding assistants like Cursor, which aim to streamline software development. A $9 billion valuation suggests that investors see Anysphere as a major player in the future of AI-enhanced programming, competing with established tools like GitHub Copilot. The involvement of top-tier VC firms like Thrive and a16z signals strong belief in the long-term potential of AI to reshape how developers work.
