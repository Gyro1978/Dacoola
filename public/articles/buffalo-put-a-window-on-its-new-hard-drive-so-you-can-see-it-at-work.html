Buffalo put a window on its new hard drive so you can see it at work

Buffalo has unveiled a limited-edition novelty hard drive with a window that reveals its inner mechanics. The Buffalo Skeleton Hard Disk celebrates the 50th anniversary of its parent company, Melco, and features a 4TB drive encased in machined aluminum with a gold and black finish. Only 50 units will be sold via lottery in June, priced at around $695 each. The drive includes a companion Windows app, SeekWizard, which lets users control the read arm’s movement for visual effects—though this may contribute to its short one-month warranty.

Why It Matters

This release is less about practicality and more about nostalgia and aesthetic appeal, harkening back to Buffalo’s 1998 transparent hard drive and Western Digital’s 2006 Raptor X. While novelty hard drives like this won’t replace mainstream storage solutions, they highlight how tech companies blend retro design with modern functionality to create collector’s items. The limited availability and high price tag position it as a niche product for enthusiasts rather than everyday users.
