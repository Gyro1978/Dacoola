FBI and Dutch Police Shut Down Botnet of Hacked Routers

A joint international law enforcement operation has dismantled a botnet consisting of thousands of hacked internet-connected devices, primarily routers. The FBI, Dutch National Police, and U.S. Department of Justice seized the domains of Anyproxy and 5Socks, two services accused of providing cybercriminals with access to compromised devices. Four individuals-three Russians and one Kazakh national-were indicted for allegedly running the botnet and profiting from its illegal activities.

The operation, dubbed "Operation Moonlander," revealed that the accused exploited vulnerabilities in older router models to create a network of proxies. These proxies were then sold to cybercriminals for anonymity in activities like financial fraud, DDoS attacks, and ad fraud. The botnet reportedly generated over $46 million in illicit revenue.

Key Details of the Botnet Shutdown

The indictment unsealed on Friday named Alexey Viktorovich Chertkov, Kirill Vladimirovich Morozov, Aleksandr Aleksandrovich Shishkin, and Dmitriy Rubtsov as the operators behind Anyproxy and 5Socks. Prosecutors allege that the group marketed their services on cybercriminal forums, offering residential IP addresses to mask malicious traffic.

Black Lotus Labs, a cybersecurity research team, assisted in tracking the botnet. According to their findings, the network averaged around 1,000 active proxies weekly across more than 80 countries. Ryan English, a researcher at Black Lotus Labs, confirmed that the majority of compromised devices were outdated routers with known security flaws.

How the Botnet Operated

The botnet functioned by hijacking vulnerable routers and redirecting internet traffic through them. This allowed cybercriminals to appear as legitimate residential users, bypassing security measures designed to detect suspicious activity. The indictment highlighted that the conspirators specifically targeted older router models with unpatched vulnerabilities, making them easy prey for exploitation.
