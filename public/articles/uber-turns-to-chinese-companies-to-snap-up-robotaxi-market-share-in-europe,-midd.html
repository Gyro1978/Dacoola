Uber turns to Chinese companies to snap up robotaxi market share in Europe, Middle East

Uber has announced its third partnership with a Chinese autonomous vehicle company, teaming up with Guangzhou-based Pony AI to launch robotaxi services. Pony AI, which went public on the Nasdaq last year at a $5.25 billion valuation, marks Uber’s latest move to dominate the emerging robotaxi sector globally.

Why It Matters

Uber’s repeated collaborations with Chinese AV firms highlight its strategy to fast-track expansion in Europe and the Middle East, leveraging China’s advanced autonomous driving technology. This move intensifies competition in the robotaxi space, where rivals like Waymo and Cruise are also vying for dominance. By aligning with well-funded players like Pony AI, Uber could accelerate regulatory approvals and deployment, potentially reshaping urban mobility in key markets.
