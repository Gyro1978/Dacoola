Hundreds of e-commerce sites hacked in supply-chain attack

Published on May 05, 2025 by AI News Team   |   View Original Source
<h2>Hundreds of e-commerce sites hacked in supply-chain attack</h2> <p>Hundreds of e-commerce sites, including one owned by a $40 billion multinational company, were infected with malware that executes malicious code in visitors' browsers, stealing payment card details and sensitive data. Security firm Sansec revealed the attack stemmed from compromised software providers, with malware lying dormant for six years before activating recently. At least 500 sites were affected, though the actual number could be double. Global remediation efforts remain limited. </p> <h3>Why It Matters</h3> <p>This attack highlights the growing risk of supply-chain vulnerabilities in e-commerce ecosystems. By targeting software providers, hackers can infect multiple downstream businesses at once, amplifying the damage. The malware’s long dormancy suggests sophisticated planning, making detection harder. With payment data at risk, affected businesses face reputational and financial fallout, while customers may lose trust in online transactions. The incident underscores the need for stronger third-party security audits and real-time threat monitoring.</p>
