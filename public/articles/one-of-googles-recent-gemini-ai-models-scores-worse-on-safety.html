One of Google’s recent Gemini AI models scores worse on safety

Google’s latest AI model, Gemini 2.5 Flash, has demonstrated a concerning AI model safety decline in internal benchmarking tests. According to a technical report released this week, the model is more prone to generating text that violates Google’s safety guidelines compared to its predecessor, Gemini 2.0 Flash. The findings highlight potential risks in deploying newer iterations of AI systems without rigorous safety evaluations.

The report specifically notes that Gemini 2.5 Flash performed worse on two key metrics related to text-to-text safety evaluations. While Google has not disclosed the exact nature of these violations, the regression suggests that improvements in other areas may have inadvertently compromised safety protocols. This development raises questions about the trade-offs between model performance and ethical safeguards in AI development.

As AI models grow more advanced, maintaining safety standards becomes increasingly critical. Google’s acknowledgment of this setback underscores the challenges in balancing innovation with responsible AI deployment. The company has yet to comment on whether corrective measures will be implemented to address the decline in safety performance.
