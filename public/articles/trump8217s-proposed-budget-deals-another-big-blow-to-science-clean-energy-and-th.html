Trump’s Proposed Budget Deals Another Big Blow to Science, Clean Energy, and the Environment

President Donald Trump’s 2026 budget proposal includes drastic cuts to federal funding for renewable energy, climate research, and environmental protections. The plan would eliminate $15 billion for clean energy initiatives, halve the Environmental Protection Agency’s budget, and slash nearly $18 billion from the National Institutes of Health. Additional cuts target NOAA, FEMA, and EPA programs, including climate education and toxic waste cleanup. Critics, including the Union of Concerned Scientists, condemned the budget’s language as "racist, anti-science, petty, and cruel."

Why It Matters

These Trump budget cuts could significantly hinder progress on climate action, public health, and environmental safety. The proposed reductions come amid increasing climate-related disasters and growing scientific consensus on the need for urgent action. By dismantling key agencies and programs, the budget risks leaving communities vulnerable to pollution, extreme weather, and health crises. The aggressive rollback of climate funding also reverses years of bipartisan efforts to transition toward clean energy, signaling a broader ideological shift away from science-based policymaking.
