New Android Spyware Targets Russian Military Personnel on the Front Lines

Russian military personnel stationed in Ukraine are being targeted by a newly discovered Android spyware strain designed to steal contacts and track their locations. The malware is embedded in a modified version of Alpine Quest, a mapping app popular among hunters, athletes, and soldiers for its offline topographical map features. The Android spyware targeting military personnel is distributed via a Telegram channel and unofficial app stores, luring victims with a free version of Alpine Quest Pro, which is normally a paid service.

The malicious app, identified as Android.Spy.1292.origin, closely mimics the legitimate Alpine Quest software to avoid detection. According to researchers at Russian cybersecurity firm Dr.Web, the trojanized app functions normally while secretly exfiltrating sensitive data. The attackers exploit the app’s utility in war zones, where reliable offline mapping tools are critical for navigation and operations.

Looks Like the Real Thing

The malware’s deceptive packaging makes it difficult for users to distinguish it from the genuine Alpine Quest app. By offering a "free" Pro version, the attackers increase the likelihood of successful infections among military personnel seeking cost-effective solutions. This tactic highlights the growing sophistication of cyber threats in conflict zones, where digital espionage can have immediate real-world consequences.
