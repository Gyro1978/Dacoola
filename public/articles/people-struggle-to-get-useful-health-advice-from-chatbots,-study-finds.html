People struggle to get useful health advice from chatbots, study finds

With overburdened healthcare systems leading to long wait times and rising costs, many people are turning to AI-powered chatbots like ChatGPT for medical self-diagnosis. A recent survey found that about 1 in 6 American adults use chatbots for health advice at least monthly. However, relying too heavily on these tools poses significant AI health advice risks, as their outputs can be unreliable or misleading.

Why It Matters

The growing reliance on AI chatbots for medical guidance reflects broader challenges in healthcare accessibility, but this trend comes with serious concerns. Misdiagnoses or incorrect advice from AI could lead to delayed treatment or harmful self-medication.
