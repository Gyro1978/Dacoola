Time Saved by AI Offset by New Work Created, Study Suggests

A recent study analyzing Denmark’s labor market in 2023 and 2024 found that generative AI tools like ChatGPT have yet to significantly alter wages or employment levels. Despite rapid adoption in certain industries, the AI labor impact has been minimal, with no measurable changes in earnings or working hours across 11 occupations, including accounting, software development, and customer support. The research, conducted by economists from the University of Chicago and the University of Copenhagen, examined data from 25,000 workers and 7,000 workplaces.

The working paper, titled "Large Language Models, Small Labor Market Effects," highlights that while AI chatbots were widely adopted—often with employer encouragement—their use did not translate into meaningful productivity gains or job displacement. The study’s statistical analysis ruled out average wage or hour changes exceeding 1%, suggesting that time saved by AI was likely offset by new tasks or inefficiencies.

Significance

These findings provide an early empirical assessment of AI’s real-world economic effects, challenging assumptions about rapid workforce transformation. The study underscores that while AI adoption is growing, its measurable impact on labor markets may take longer to materialize than previously anticipated.
