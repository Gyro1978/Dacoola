Is Duolingo the Face of an AI Jobs Crisis?

Duolingo announced plans to replace contractors with AI, positioning itself as an "AI-first" company. Journalist Brian Merchant highlighted this as evidence that the AI job displacement crisis is already unfolding. A former contractor revealed this isn’t a new policy, suggesting a broader trend of workforce automation in the tech sector.

Why It Matters

This move underscores the accelerating adoption of AI in roles traditionally filled by humans, particularly in content and language services. While AI-driven efficiency benefits companies, it raises urgent questions about job security and reskilling needs. Duolingo’s shift could signal a wider industry pivot, forcing policymakers and businesses to address the socioeconomic impact of automation sooner rather than later.
