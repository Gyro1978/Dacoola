Salesforce takes aim at ‘jagged intelligence’ in push for more reliable AI

Salesforce has unveiled new AI research aimed at tackling "jagged intelligence," a term referring to inconsistencies in AI performance. The company introduced benchmarks, models, and guardrails designed to make enterprise AI agents more intelligent, trusted, and reliable for business applications. This initiative seeks to improve Salesforce AI reliability by ensuring more consistent and dependable outputs.

Why It Matters

The push to address "jagged intelligence" highlights a critical challenge in enterprise AI adoption—unpredictable performance. By establishing new benchmarks and safeguards, Salesforce is positioning itself as a leader in developing AI solutions businesses can trust. This move could set industry standards, encouraging broader AI integration in workflows where reliability is non-negotiable.
