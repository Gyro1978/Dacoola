Apple Blocks Fortnite in US and EU Amid Escalating Apple-Epic Dispute

Published on May 16, 2025 by AI News Team   |   View Original Source
<h2>Apple Blocks Fortnite in US and EU Amid Escalating Apple-Epic Dispute</h2> <p>Epic Games has accused Apple of blocking Fortnite from the U.S. and E.U. App Stores, escalating the ongoing Apple-Epic dispute. Despite a recent legal victory allowing external payments in Apple’s U.S. App Store, Epic claims Apple has delayed approving Fortnite’s resubmission, forcing the game offline for iOS users worldwide. </p> <!-- DACCOOLA_IN_ARTICLE_AD_HERE --> <h3>The Latest Chapter in the Apple-Epic Dispute</h3> <p>Epic Games attempted to resubmit Fortnite to the U.S. App Store on May 9, 2025, following a court ruling that required Apple to permit alternative payment methods. However, Apple reportedly delayed approval for a week, prompting Epic to withdraw and resubmit the app on May 14. According to Epic CEO Tim Sweeney, the delay disrupted Fortnite’s weekly content updates, which require simultaneous releases across all platforms. </p> <p>On May 16, Fortnite’s official X account announced that Apple had rejected the latest submission, effectively blocking the game from both the U.S. and E.U. markets. “Now, sadly, Fortnite on iOS will be offline worldwide until Apple unblocks it,” the post stated. </p> <h4>What’s Next for Fortnite Players?</h4> <p>With Fortnite unavailable on iOS, players in the U.S. and E.U. must rely on other platforms, such as consoles or PC, to access the game. The move raises questions about Apple’s compliance with recent regulatory changes, particularly in the E.U., where the Digital Markets Act (DMA) mandates greater openness in app distribution.</p>
