Gemini AI Upgrade Expands to Android Devices, WearOS, and Cars

Google has announced a major expansion of its Gemini AI assistant, bringing advanced conversational capabilities to WearOS smartwatches, Android Auto, and Google TV. The Gemini AI upgrade, unveiled ahead of Google I/O, promises hands-free assistance across multiple devices, enhancing productivity and convenience for users.

Key Innovations & Market Impact

The Gemini AI upgrade introduces seamless integration across Google’s ecosystem, starting with WearOS smartwatches later this year. Users can now set reminders, get workout assistance, or even ask for help while cooking-all without needing their phones. This hands-free functionality is particularly useful for multitasking scenarios, such as when users are mid-workout or have messy hands.

Android Auto is also receiving a significant boost, replacing Google Assistant with Gemini. Drivers can now ask for complex directions, such as finding a charging station near a park, or get real-time message summaries and translations. The upgrade leverages Gemini Live’s multi-turn conversation feature, allowing for more natural interactions, whether users want news updates or casual chats.

Google TV is next in line, with Gemini set to enhance content discovery. During a CES 2025 demo, the AI successfully recommended kid-friendly movies similar to Jurassic Park, showcasing its ability to understand nuanced requests.

Extended Reality Integration

Google and Samsung are collaborating to bring Gemini to Android XR, a platform for extended reality devices like headsets and glasses. When Samsung’s first headset launches, users will experience AI-powered assistance in an immersive environment, with contextual information displayed around them.

Pros & Cons

Pros
  • **Hands-free convenience** for WearOS and Android Auto users.
  • **Enhanced conversational AI** with multi-turn interactions.
Cons
  • Rollout is staggered, with Android Auto support arriving later.
  • Dependent on device compatibility, limiting early adoption.

Frequently Asked Questions

When will Gemini be available on WearOS?

Google confirmed Gemini will launch on WearOS smartwatches later this year.

Can Gemini replace Google Assistant in Android Auto?

Yes, Gemini will fully replace Google Assistant in Android Auto, offering more advanced conversational features.

Will Gemini work on all Android XR devices?

Initially, Gemini will debut on Samsung’s upcoming headset, with broader compatibility planned for future devices.
