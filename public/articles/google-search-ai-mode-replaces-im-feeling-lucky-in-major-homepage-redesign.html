Google Search AI Mode Replaces 'I'm Feeling Lucky' in Major Homepage Redesign

Google is testing a significant redesign of its Search homepage, replacing the long-standing "I'm Feeling Lucky" button with "AI Mode," its experimental AI-powered search feature. The change, currently rolling out to select users in Google Labs, may not see a public release but comes just ahead of Google I/O, where further AI search updates are expected.

This move highlights Google's urgency to adapt as AI tools like ChatGPT impact traditional search behavior-Apple recently noted the first-ever decline in Google Searches via Safari last month.

A Strategic Shift Toward AI-Powered Search

Google rarely alters its Search homepage, making this test particularly noteworthy. The "I'm Feeling Lucky" button, introduced in 1998, bypassed search results entirely, directly taking users to the top-ranked page. Its potential replacement with AI Mode-launched earlier in May-signals a deeper integration of generative AI into core search functions.

The timing aligns with growing competition from AI-first tools. An Apple executive recently testified that Google Searches on Safari dropped for the first time, attributing the trend to ChatGPT and similar platforms. By prioritizing AI Mode Google Search, Google may be countering this threat while streamlining access to its most advanced features.

What This Means for Users

If implemented, the change would place AI-powered answers front and center, reducing reliance on traditional blue links. However, the feature remains experimental, and Google has not confirmed a full rollout. The test suggests the company is willing to retire nostalgic elements like "I'm Feeling Lucky" to stay competitive in the AI era.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Google removing "I'm Feeling Lucky" permanently?

Not yet-this is a limited test in Google Labs. The company hasn't announced a full replacement.

How does AI Mode differ from standard Google Search?

AI Mode generates summarized answers using generative AI, while traditional search displays links. It’s designed for complex queries.

Will this change affect SEO?

Potentially-if AI Mode prioritizes direct answers over links, publishers may need to adapt content strategies.
