Sam Altman Lifestyle: OpenAI CEO's Olive Oil Misuse Sparks Social Media Buzz

OpenAI CEO Sam Altman has found himself in an unexpected spotlight-not for AI advancements, but for his unconventional use of premium olive oil. During a recent "Lunch with the FT" feature, Altman's cooking habits revealed a surprising oversight: he used Graza's high-end "drizzle" oil for sautéing instead of its intended finishing purpose. The Financial Times humorously dubbed it an "offense to horticulture," sparking widespread amusement online.

The Olive Oil Controversy Explained

Graza, a millennial-favorite olive oil brand, markets two distinct varieties: "sizzle" for cooking and "drizzle" for finishing dishes. Altman, however, opted to sauté garlic in the pricier "drizzle" oil, despite the "sizzle" bottle being within arm's reach. The blunder drew comparisons to Silicon Valley's notorious spending habits-jokingly suggesting that Altman might treat venture capital funds with similar disregard.

The incident highlights a broader cultural fascination with the lifestyles of tech elites. Altman, who recently led OpenAI through a record $40 billion funding round, is no stranger to scrutiny. Yet, this lighthearted critique offers a rare glimpse into his off-duty persona.

Silicon Valley's Culinary Culture

The episode also underscores the intersection of tech culture and consumer trends. Graza's sleek, Instagram-friendly packaging has made it a status symbol among millennials, much like Altman's own brand of influence. While the misuse of olive oil may seem trivial, it resonates as a metaphor for excess in an industry known for its lavish spending-OpenAI reportedly lost $5 billion last year despite its premium-priced ChatGPT Pro subscriptions.
