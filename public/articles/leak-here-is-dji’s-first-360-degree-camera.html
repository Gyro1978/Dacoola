Leak: Here is DJI’s first 360-degree camera

A prototype of DJI’s first 360-degree camera, the Osmo 360, has been leaked online, revealing key details before an official announcement. Shared by leakers Hakasushi and Igor Bogdanov, the images show a compact, square-shaped dual-lens camera with a touchscreen, interchangeable batteries, and a tripod mount featuring copper contacts. The device includes Wi-Fi 6, Bluetooth 5.1, and microSD storage, though critical specs like resolution remain unknown. Internally, it mirrors Insta360’s design, using 45-degree mirrors to redirect light to the sensors. However, some speculate DJI may not release it, especially in the U.S., due to unusually tight secrecy.

Why It Matters

This DJI 360 camera leak suggests the company is entering a competitive market dominated by Insta360 and GoPro. If launched, the Osmo 360 could shake up the 360-camera space with DJI’s reputation for high-quality imaging tech. The compact design and modular accessories hint at versatility, appealing to creators. However, DJI’s silence and geopolitical challenges raise doubts about its release. For now, the leak offers a tantalizing glimpse into DJI’s potential expansion beyond drones and action cams.
