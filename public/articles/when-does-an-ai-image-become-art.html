When Does an AI Image Become Art?

AI-generated images are now widely used in advertising, social media, and entertainment, thanks to models like Midjourney and DALL-E. However, AI's role in art dates back decades, as seen in Harold Cohen’s AARON, an expert system developed in the 1970s that emulated human artistic decision-making. Christiane Paul, curator at the Whitney Museum of American Art, highlights how AI in art has evolved, contrasting Cohen’s rule-based approach with today’s statistically driven models.

Why It Matters

The discussion around AI in art raises key questions about authorship, creativity, and the role of technology. Unlike early systems like AARON, where artists had full control, today’s AI models operate within corporate frameworks, limiting artistic agency. Paul emphasizes that true AI art critically engages with the technology—questioning biases, curating datasets, and refining outputs—rather than merely generating images from prompts. This distinction is crucial as AI continues to blur the line between tool and collaborator in creative fields.
