Google is going to let kids use its Gemini AI

Google is rolling out access to its Gemini AI for kids under 13 via Family Link parental controls, according to an email sent to parents. Children will be able to use the AI for tasks like homework help and storytelling, with assurances that their data won’t train AI models. However, Google warns parents that Gemini may produce errors or inappropriate content, advising them to discuss AI limitations with their kids. Parents can disable access via Family Link and will receive notifications when their child first uses Gemini.

Why It Matters

This move reflects growing AI integration into education and child-friendly tech, but it also highlights concerns about misinformation and digital safety. While Google’s safeguards—like parental controls and data privacy measures—are a step forward, past incidents with AI chatbots (such as Character.ai’s misleading interactions) underscore the need for caution. Parents must balance AI’s educational benefits with active supervision to ensure kids engage with these tools responsibly.
