Microsoft announces a Gears of War remaster – and it’s coming to PlayStation, too

Microsoft has announced Gears of War: Reloaded, a remaster of the original game launching on August 26th for Xbox Series X/S, PlayStation, and PC at $39.99. The game features 4K resolution, 120 FPS support, cross-play, and includes all content from Gears of War: Ultimate Edition, including bonus campaign acts, multiplayer maps, and unlockable cosmetics. Players who owned Ultimate Edition before the announcement will receive Reloaded as a free upgrade.

Why It Matters

This marks a significant shift for Microsoft, bringing a once Xbox-exclusive franchise to PlayStation—a move that highlights the company’s growing focus on cross-platform accessibility. The inclusion of cross-play and free upgrades for existing owners also signals a player-friendly approach, potentially setting a new standard for remastered releases. With Gears of War: E-Day also in development, Microsoft is clearly doubling down on revitalizing the franchise for both longtime fans and new audiences.
