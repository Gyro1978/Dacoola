Microsoft Embraces Google’s AI Agent Communication Protocol for Cross-Platform Collaboration

Microsoft has announced its adoption of Google’s AI agent communication protocol, Agent2Agent (A2A), integrating it into Azure AI Foundry and Copilot Studio. This move enables AI agents built on Microsoft’s platforms to interact seamlessly with external agents, including those hosted on Google’s ecosystem. By joining the A2A working group, Microsoft aims to foster a collaborative, interoperable AI landscape where agents can securely exchange goals and execute tasks across different clouds and applications.

Unpacking the Impact of A2A Integration

The adoption of A2A marks a significant step toward standardizing AI agent communication protocols in the industry. Microsoft’s support for A2A allows developers to create multi-agent workflows that span internal systems, partner tools, and third-party services while maintaining governance and security. For example, a Microsoft AI agent could schedule a meeting while a Google agent drafts the invitation—showcasing the potential for seamless cross-platform automation.

This aligns with broader industry efforts to establish shared agent protocols, following Microsoft’s earlier adoption of Anthropic’s MCP standard. With enterprises increasingly experimenting with AI agents—65% according to a KPMG survey—interoperability is becoming critical. The AI agent market is projected to grow from $7.84 billion in 2025 to $52.62 billion by 2030, underscoring the importance of standardized communication frameworks.

The Technology Behind A2A

Google’s A2A protocol enables AI agents to work together across different environments by providing a set of interoperable components. Agents can securely exchange objectives and invoke actions, ensuring smooth collaboration. Microsoft’s integration means Azure AI and Copilot Studio users can now leverage external agents for complex workflows, enhancing productivity and flexibility.

Pros & Cons

Pros
