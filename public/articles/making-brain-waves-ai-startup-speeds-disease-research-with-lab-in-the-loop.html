Making Brain Waves: AI Startup Speeds Disease Research With Lab in the Loop

Published on April 22, 2025 by AI News Team   |   View Original Source
<h2>Making Brain Waves: AI Startup Speeds Disease Research With Lab in the Loop</h2> <p>BrainStorm Therapeutics, a San Diego-based startup, is leveraging <strong>AI drug discovery</strong> to tackle neurological disorders affecting over a billion people worldwide. By combining AI-powered computational models with lab experiments using brain organoids—3D cell clusters derived from patient stem cells—the company aims to reduce the 93% failure rate of brain disease drug candidates in clinical trials. Their "lab in the loop" approach iteratively refines drug development by integrating AI insights with real-world organoid testing. </p> <p>The startup’s platform, powered by NVIDIA GPUs and the BioNeMo Framework, enables rapid screening of thousands of drug molecules daily. BrainStorm’s organoids mimic human brain activity, including brain waves detectable via EEG-like scans, allowing researchers to simulate clinical trials in a dish. This method has already identified Donepezil, an Alzheimer’s drug, as a potential treatment for Rett syndrome—a breakthrough that progressed to FDA clearance for Phase 2 trials in just nine months. </p> <h3>Accelerating Drug Discovery Research</h3> <p>BrainStorm’s AI models analyze genetic and cellular data to identify promising drug targets, particularly for Parkinson’s disease, where dopamine neuron loss is a key challenge. By fine-tuning NVIDIA’s Geneformer model with patient-derived organoid data, the team maps disease pathways to uncover therapies that could slow or reverse progression. Future plans include multimodal AI models integrating sequencing, imaging, and EEG data to enhance precision medicine for rare and common neurological conditions.</p>
