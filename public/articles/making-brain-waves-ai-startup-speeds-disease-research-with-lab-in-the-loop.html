Making Brain Waves: AI Startup Speeds Disease Research With Lab in the Loop

BrainStorm Therapeutics, a San Diego-based startup, is leveraging AI drug discovery to tackle neurological disorders affecting over a billion people worldwide. By combining AI-powered computational models with lab experiments using brain organoids—3D cell clusters derived from patient stem cells—the company aims to lower the 93% failure rate of brain disease drug candidates in clinical trials. Their "lab in the loop" approach iteratively refines drug development by integrating AI insights with real-world organoid testing.

The startup’s platform, powered by NVIDIA GPUs and the BioNeMo Framework, enables rapid screening of thousands of drug molecules daily. BrainStorm’s organoids mimic human brain waves, allowing researchers to study disease mechanisms and test therapies in a controlled environment before human trials. This method recently identified Donepezil, an Alzheimer’s drug, as a potential treatment for Rett syndrome—progressing from lab testing to FDA-cleared clinical trials in just nine months.

Accelerating Drug Discovery Research

BrainStorm’s AI models analyze genetic and cellular data to pinpoint drug targets, particularly for Parkinson’s disease, where dopamine neuron loss is a key challenge. By fine-tuning models like Geneformer with patient-derived organoid data, the team accelerates the identification of disease-modifying treatments. Future plans include multimodal AI models integrating EEG scans and imaging to enhance precision medicine for rare and common neurological conditions.
