Beyond A2A and MCP: How LOKA’s Universal Agent Identity Layer Changes the Game

Researchers at Carnegie Mellon University have introduced the LOKA protocol, a proposed AI agent identity standard designed to assign identities and intentions to AI agents. This framework aims to standardize how agents identify themselves and communicate their objectives, potentially improving interoperability and trust in multi-agent systems.

Why It Matters

The LOKA protocol could address a critical gap in AI agent ecosystems: the lack of a universal identity layer. Currently, agents often operate without clear identification or purpose, leading to inefficiencies and security risks. By standardizing agent identities and intentions, LOKA may enable smoother collaboration between AI systems, reduce miscommunication, and enhance accountability—key steps toward more reliable and scalable AI deployments.
