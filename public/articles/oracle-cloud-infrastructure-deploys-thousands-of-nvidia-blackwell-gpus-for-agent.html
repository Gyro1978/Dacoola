Oracle Cloud Infrastructure Deploys Thousands of NVIDIA Blackwell GPUs for Agentic AI and Reasoning Models

Oracle has launched its first wave of liquid-cooled NVIDIA Blackwell GPUs deployment, specifically the NVIDIA GB200 NVL72 racks, in its data centers. These high-performance GPUs are now available for customers on NVIDIA DGX Cloud and Oracle Cloud Infrastructure (OCI), enabling the development of advanced reasoning models and AI agents. The deployment includes cutting-edge NVIDIA Quantum-2 InfiniBand and Spectrum-X Ethernet networking to ensure scalable, low-latency performance.

OCI is among the first cloud providers to deploy NVIDIA GB200 NVL72 systems, with plans to expand into one of the worldâ€™s largest Blackwell clusters, scaling beyond 100,000 GPUs. The infrastructure supports OCI Superclusters, designed to meet the surging demand for AI inference and accelerated computing. This move aligns with the rapid pace of AI innovation, as companies like OpenAI continue releasing new reasoning models.

The NVIDIA GB200 NVL72 platform combines 36 Grace CPUs and 72 Blackwell GPUs per rack, delivering exceptional efficiency for agentic AI applications. OCI offers flexible deployment options, including public, government, and sovereign clouds, as well as customer-owned data centers through OCI Dedicated Region and OCI Alloy. Major enterprises, government agencies, and cloud providers are already planning to leverage these systems for AI workloads.

Significance

This deployment marks a significant step in transforming cloud data centers into AI factories capable of large-scale intelligence production. With NVIDIA DGX Cloud integration, enterprises gain access to optimized AI development tools, accelerating advancements in autonomous vehicles, chip design, and AI reasoning models.
