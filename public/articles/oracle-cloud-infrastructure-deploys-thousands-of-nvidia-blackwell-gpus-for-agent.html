Oracle Cloud Infrastructure Deploys Thousands of NVIDIA Blackwell GPUs for Agentic AI and Reasoning Models

Oracle has launched its first wave of liquid-cooled NVIDIA GB200 NVL72 racks, deploying thousands of NVIDIA Blackwell GPUs across its data centers. These GPUs are now available for customer use on NVIDIA DGX Cloud and Oracle Cloud Infrastructure (OCI), enabling the development of advanced reasoning models and AI agents. The deployment includes high-speed NVIDIA Quantum-2 InfiniBand and Spectrum-X Ethernet networking, ensuring scalable, low-latency performance for AI workloads.

OCI is among the first cloud providers to adopt the NVIDIA GB200 NVL72 systems, with plans to expand into one of the worldâ€™s largest Blackwell clusters, scaling beyond 100,000 GPUs. The infrastructure supports OCI Superclusters, designed to meet the surging demand for AI inference and accelerated computing. Additionally, Oracle offers flexible deployment options, including public, government, and sovereign clouds, as well as OCI Dedicated Region and OCI Alloy for on-premises solutions.

Significance

The deployment marks a major step in transforming cloud data centers into AI factories, capable of producing intelligence at scale. Major enterprises, government agencies, and regional cloud providers are already preparing to leverage these systems for AI-driven applications, from autonomous vehicles to chip design. With NVIDIA DGX Cloud now live on OCI, developers gain access to optimized AI tools and services, accelerating innovation in agentic AI and reasoning models.
