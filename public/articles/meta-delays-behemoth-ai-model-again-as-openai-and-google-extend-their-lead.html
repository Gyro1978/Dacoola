Meta Delays 'Behemoth' AI Model Again as OpenAI and Google Extend Their Lead

Published on May 16, 2025 by AI News Team   |   View Original Source

Meta has postponed the release of its highly anticipated "Behemoth" large language model (LLM) for the second time this year, potentially pushing its debut to fall 2025 or later. The delay comes just weeks after Meta claimed Behemoth outperformed rivals like GPT-4.5 and Gemini 2.0 Pro on STEM benchmarks, raising questions about the company's ability to compete in the rapidly evolving AI landscape.

Internal documents reveal engineers are struggling to achieve meaningful improvements over existing models, despite Meta's public confidence. The setback hands OpenAI and Google additional time to solidify their positions as leaders in generative AI, while Meta scrambles to refine a model originally slated for release at its LlamaCon developer conference last month.

Behind the Behemoth Delay: Technical Hurdles and Internal Doubts

The Wall Street Journal reports Meta engineers face unexpected challenges enhancing Behemoth's capabilities, particularly in maintaining performance consistency across diverse tasks. This contradicts Meta's April claims that the model already surpassed competitors in specialized STEM evaluations - a discrepancy suggesting potential benchmarking cherry-picking.

Sources indicate the team is wrestling with:
Diminishing returns from increased parameter scaling (reportedly exceeding 1 trillion)
 High computational costs for marginal quality gains
* Difficulty translating narrow benchmark wins to broad real-world superiority

The Benchmarking Paradox

While Meta touts Behemoth's STEM test results, internal critics argue these metrics don't reflect overall usability. The model reportedly struggles with:
1. Multimodal integration (text-to-image/code transitions)
2. Context retention beyond 128k tokens
3. Real-time inference speed

Competitive Fallout: Meta Loses Ground in Three-Car Race

The delay exacerbates Meta's growing gap with rivals:

Company Recent Release Key Advantage
OpenAI GPT-4.5 (May 2025) Enterprise-grade reliability
Google Gemini 2.0 Pro (April 2025) Seamless Workspace integration
Meta Llama 4 variants (Q2 2025) Open-weight models

Analysts note Meta's consumer-facing AI features (Facebook post drafting, Instagram editing tools) rely on older Llama 3 infrastructure, while competitors deploy cutting-edge models. The postponed release could cost Meta crucial developer mindshare as OpenAI's GPT Store and Google's Gemini API gain traction.

Strategic Implications: More Than Just a Schedule Slip

The Behemoth delay reflects deeper challenges:

Resource Allocation
Meta's AR/VR investments continue diverting AI talent, with 37% of Reality Labs staff holding ML expertise that could bolster LLM development.

Open vs. Closed Model Debate
Meta's open-weight approach (used for Llama models) may hinder Behemoth's competitiveness against proprietary systems like GPT-4.5, as sharing weights limits monetization options that fund R&D.

Regulatory Headwinds
EU AI Act compliance requirements for "high-risk" models could further complicate Behemoth's rollout, especially regarding:
- Training data transparency
- Real-time monitoring mandates
- Third-party auditing

Pros and Cons

Pros
  • Rigorous validation: Delay suggests Meta won't release underperforming models, maintaining quality standards.
  • STEM specialization: Benchmarks indicate potential niche superiority in technical domains.
  • Future-proof architecture: "Teacher model" design could enable faster iteration of subsequent LLMs.
Cons
  • Market perception: Repeated delays damage credibility in hyper-competitive AI sector.
  • Integration lag: Meta's apps may fall behind in AI features compared to rivals.
  • Resource intensity: Behemoth's size may limit accessibility for smaller developers.

Final Assessment: Can Meta Regain Its Footing?

The Behemoth delay underscores Meta's structural challenges in keeping pace with better-funded, more focused AI competitors. While the model's eventual release could shake up STEM-focused applications, the postponement gives OpenAI and Google 6-9 additional months to entrench their positions.

Meta's best path forward may involve:
Prioritizing specific use cases over general superiority
 Accelerating integration of existing Llama 4 models
* Partnering with academic institutions for specialized training

The coming months will reveal whether this setback becomes a strategic recalibration or the start of a widening gap in the AI arms race.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does Behemoth's delay impact Meta's AI strategy?

The postponement forces Meta to rely on smaller Llama 4 variants longer, potentially ceding ground in premium AI services. It also delays their "teacher model" approach for training future generations of LLMs.

What advantages does Behemoth still hold over GPT-4.5 and Gemini 2.0?

Early benchmarks suggest strengths in technical domains like mathematics and coding, though real-world performance remains unproven. Its open-weight architecture could appeal to researchers if released.

Could this delay benefit Meta by avoiding rushed releases?

Potentially. Google's rushed Gemini 1.0 launch demonstrated the reputational risks of premature releases. However, extended delays risk making the technology obsolete upon arrival.
