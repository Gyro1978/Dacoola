Elton John and Dua Lipa Lead Call for AI Protections in Creative Industries

Over 400 British musicians, writers, and artists, including Elton John, Dua Lipa, and Sir Paul McCartney, have urged Prime Minister Keir Starmer to update copyright laws to safeguard their work from artificial intelligence. In a letter, they warn that without legal protections, their creations risk being exploited by tech firms, threatening the UK's status as a creative leader. The group supports an amendment to the Data (Use and Access) Bill, requiring AI developers to disclose when copyrighted material is used for training models.

A government spokesperson acknowledged the need to balance innovation with creator rights, stating that no changes would proceed without ensuring fairness. Meanwhile, critics argue stricter regulations could drive AI development overseas, harming the UK economy.

Key Demands and Industry Reactions

The artists' letter highlights growing tensions between AI advancements and intellectual property rights. They argue that generative AI systems, which produce text, music, and art from prompts, often rely on copyrighted content without compensation. Baroness Beeban Kidron's proposed amendment aims to establish licensing agreements between creators and tech companies, ensuring transparency and fair use.

However, Julia Willemyns of the Centre for British Progress warns that restrictive laws might stifle domestic AI innovation while failing to prevent foreign firms from accessing UK content. The debate follows a February protest by artists like Annie Lennox, who released a silent album opposing looser copyright rules.

Government Response and Next Steps

The government has paused its initial "opt-out" proposal, which would have allowed AI firms to use online content unless creators explicitly objected. Nobel laureate Kazuo Ishiguro criticized the plan as favoring corporations over artists. While officials reconsider their approach, Baroness Kidron emphasizes that transparency measures could foster a licensing market, positioning the UK as a global AI leader.
