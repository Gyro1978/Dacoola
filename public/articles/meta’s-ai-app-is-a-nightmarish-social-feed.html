Meta’s AI app is a nightmarish social feed

Meta’s new AI app combines the visual clutter of Pinterest with the worst aspects of Threads, topped with AI-generated prompts. Unlike private AI chatbots like ChatGPT, Meta AI allows users to share their AI-generated results publicly with just two clicks. The feed is filled with bizarre experiments, from AI-generated images to trivial queries, highlighting how users struggle to find meaningful uses for generative AI. Meta’s VP of product claims the social aspect is meant to inspire AI newcomers—but the result is a chaotic, often nonsensical experience.

Why It Matters

This Meta AI app critique underscores a growing tension in AI adoption: while companies push for broader engagement, many users still treat AI as a novelty rather than a tool. The feed’s randomness reflects the broader confusion around generative AI’s practical applications. If Meta wants to position its AI as useful, it may need to rethink how it encourages meaningful interaction—otherwise, the platform risks becoming a digital curiosity cabinet rather than a productivity enhancer.
