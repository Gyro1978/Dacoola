Bill Gates Directs Gates Foundation to Spend Entire $200 Billion by 2045

Bill Gates has announced that his foundation must exhaust its $200 billion endowment by 2045, accelerating its philanthropic mission. The Microsoft co-founder, who plans to donate 99% of his $107 billion fortune, revised the foundation’s original timeline, which was tied to his death. The Gates Foundation, now 25 years old, has already spent over $100 billion on global health, education, and development.

A Strategic Shift in Philanthropy

Gates described the decision as part of a personal reflection, coinciding with milestones including his 70th birthday and Microsoft’s 50th anniversary. The revised deadline ensures the foundation will operate for just 20 more years, a significant departure from its previous indefinite lifespan. With an expected annual expenditure of $9 billion, the foundation’s accelerated spending could amplify its impact on global health crises like malaria and gender inequality.

Warren Buffett’s contributions may still surpass Gates’s as the largest philanthropic gift in U.S. history. However, the Gates Foundation’s revised timeline underscores a growing trend among billionaires to allocate wealth more aggressively during their lifetimes.

Impact on Global Aid

The announcement comes amid U.S. foreign aid cuts, with agencies like USAID facing reduced funding. The Gates Foundation’s sustained $9 billion yearly spending could help fill gaps in low-income countries, particularly in healthcare and education.
