Tesla Leadership Denial: Board Stands Firm Behind Elon Musk

Tesla has strongly denied reports that its board contacted headhunters to replace CEO Elon Musk. The Wall Street Journal claimed the board sought a successor due to concerns over Musk's focus on his role in the Trump administration and Tesla's declining stock performance. However, Tesla Chair Robyn Denholm dismissed the report as "absolutely false," reaffirming the board's confidence in Musk's leadership.

Musk himself criticized the WSJ, calling the article a "breach of ethics" and accusing the publication of ignoring Tesla's preemptive denial. The controversy comes as Tesla faces backlash from customers and investors over Musk's political affiliations and divided attention between Tesla, SpaceX, and his government advisory role.

Unpacking the Leadership Controversy

The Wall Street Journal's report suggested Tesla's board urged Musk to dedicate more time to the company, a claim Musk seemingly acknowledged during a recent earnings call. However, Tesla's swift denial highlights tensions between media narratives and corporate messaging. Musk's dual roles—leading Tesla while advising the Trump administration—have fueled skepticism among investors and customers alike.

Public and Investor Reactions

Some Tesla customers have boycotted the brand, citing Musk's political stance, while protests have occasionally turned destructive. Meanwhile, analysts argue that Musk's divided focus has contributed to Tesla's financial struggles. Despite this, Tesla's board remains publicly supportive, emphasizing Musk's role in the company's "exciting growth plan."

Musk’s Defense and Future Commitments

Musk has pledged to reduce his government involvement, stating he will allocate "far more time" to Tesla. Yet, his exact timeline remains unclear. His recent quip—"I wear a lot of hats"—during a White House meeting underscores the balancing act he faces. With Trump expressing a desire to retain Musk, the CEO's government role may persist beyond initial expectations.
