NVIDIA Sweeps COMPUTEX 2025 With Record Best Choice Awards Haul

NVIDIA claimed an unprecedented sweep at COMPUTEX 2025's Best Choice Awards, securing top honors across gaming, AI infrastructure, and data center categories. The tech giant's GeForce RTX 5090 GPU, GB200 NVL72 system, Cosmos world model platform, and Quantum-X Photonics InfiniBand switch all received recognition for technological innovation and market potential.

Flagship Products Earn Golden Awards

The NVIDIA GB200 NVL72 rack-scale AI system and Cosmos physical AI platform both received Golden Awards, COMPUTEX's highest distinction. The GB200 NVL72 combines 36 Grace CPUs with 72 Blackwell GPUs to deliver 1.4 exaflops of AI performance - enabling 30x faster trillion-parameter LLM inference at 25x improved energy efficiency versus previous H100 systems.

Meanwhile, the Cosmos platform is revolutionizing physical AI development by processing 20 million hours of video data in two weeks - a task requiring three years on CPU-only systems. Pretrained on 9,000 trillion tokens of robotics data, Cosmos accelerates synthetic data generation for autonomous vehicle and robotics applications.

Category Wins Span NVIDIA's Portfolio

Beyond the Golden Awards, NVIDIA dominated specialized categories:
- Gaming & Entertainment: RTX 5090 GPU with Blackwell architecture and DLSS 4
- Networking & Communication: Quantum-X Photonics InfiniBand switch with co-packaged optics
- Computer & Systems: DGX Spark desktop AI supercomputer

The awards coincide with CEO Jensen Huang's upcoming COMPUTEX keynote on May 19, where further AI advancements are expected to be unveiled. Industry analysts note the sweep reinforces NVIDIA's vertical integration from consumer GPUs to hyperscale AI infrastructure.
