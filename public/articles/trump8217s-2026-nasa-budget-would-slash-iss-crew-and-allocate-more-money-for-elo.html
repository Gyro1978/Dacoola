Trump’s 2026 NASA Budget Would Slash ISS Crew and Allocate More Money for Elon

The Trump administration has proposed cutting about a quarter of NASA’s 2026 budget, significantly reducing International Space Station (ISS) crew sizes and research capacity while reallocating funds toward Mars exploration and lunar missions. The NASA budget proposal includes a $508 million cut to the ISS, along with $2.265 billion slashed from space science missions, including the termination of the Mars Sample Return mission. Meanwhile, $1 billion is earmarked for Mars-focused programs, likely benefiting SpaceX’s Starship ambitions, and $7 billion is allocated for lunar exploration—potentially phasing out NASA’s SLS rocket and Orion capsule.

Why It Matters

This budget signals a dramatic shift in U.S. space priorities, moving away from Earth-focused research (including climate monitoring) and toward human-led Mars and Moon missions. The cuts could weaken America’s leadership in space science and international collaboration, particularly with the ISS. However, the emphasis on commercial alternatives like SpaceX aligns with growing privatization trends in space exploration. Congress will ultimately decide the final budget, but this proposal sets the stage for a contentious debate over NASA’s future direction.
