Why I Love My Brother 1034D Serger

Cath Virginia, a senior designer at The Verge, swears by her Brother 1034D Serger for sewing knitwear. Unlike traditional sewing machines, this serger uses four threads and a built-in knife to create clean, stretchable seams—ideal for T-shirts, leggings, and other knit garments. Virginia highlights its ease of use, adjustable free arm, and differential feed system, which prevents fabric ruffling. Though initially intimidated by threading four spools, she mastered it quickly thanks to clear instructions.

Why It Matters

The Brother 1034D Serger exemplifies how specialized tools can elevate craftsmanship, especially for those sewing stretchy fabrics. Its efficiency—cutting and finishing seams in one pass—saves time, while features like differential feed ensure professional results. For hobbyists and professionals alike, investing in a serger like this can bridge the gap between homemade and store-bought quality. However, as Virginia notes, it’s not a replacement for a traditional sewing machine, emphasizing the importance of choosing tools tailored to specific needs.
