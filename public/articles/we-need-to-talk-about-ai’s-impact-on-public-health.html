We Need to Talk About AI’s Impact on Public Health

Published on May 01, 2025 by AI News Team   |   View Original Source
<h2>We Need to Talk About AI’s Impact on Public Health</h2> <p>The rapid growth of AI is driving a surge in data center energy and water consumption, with U.S. electricity demand projected to reach 6.7–12% of the national total by 2028. However, the <strong>AI environmental impact</strong> goes beyond resource use—backup generators and fossil-fueled power plants emit harmful pollutants like PM2.5 and nitrogen oxides, linked to asthma, heart disease, and cognitive decline. Research estimates U.S. data centers caused $6 billion in public health damages in 2023, a figure that could rise to $20 billion by 2030. </p> <h3>Why It Matters</h3> <p>While AI’s carbon footprint is widely discussed, its health consequences remain overlooked. Air pollution is a "silent killer," responsible for 4 million premature deaths annually. Unlike carbon emissions, health risks vary by location, meaning efficiency gains alone won’t solve the problem. Demand-side strategies, like routing AI workloads to cleaner regions, could mitigate harm without major infrastructure changes. As AI becomes a public utility, integrating health considerations into its deployment is critical to balancing innovation with public well-being.</p>
