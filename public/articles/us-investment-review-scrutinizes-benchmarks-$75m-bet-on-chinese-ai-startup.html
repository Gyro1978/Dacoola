US Investment Review Scrutinizes Benchmark's $75M Bet on Chinese AI Startup

The US Treasury Department is examining Benchmark Capital's $75 million investment in Chinese AI startup Manus, according to sources cited by Semafor. The US investment review focuses on whether the deal complies with 2023 restrictions on funding Chinese tech companies. Manus recently achieved a $500 million valuation despite being an AI "wrapper" rather than a core model developer.

Legal Gray Areas in Cross-Border Tech Funding

Benchmark's legal team reportedly approved the investment by arguing Manus doesn't develop proprietary AI models - instead integrating existing ones - and is incorporated in the Cayman Islands rather than mainland China. This structure mirrors approaches used by Alibaba and other Chinese firms to attract foreign capital. However, critics like Founders Fund's Delian Asparouhov have questioned the investment's alignment with US policy goals.

The review highlights ongoing tensions between Silicon Valley's investment strategies and Washington's efforts to limit Chinese tech advancement. While Benchmark hasn't commented, the case could set precedents for how Treasury interprets investment restrictions regarding offshore entities with substantial Chinese operations.
