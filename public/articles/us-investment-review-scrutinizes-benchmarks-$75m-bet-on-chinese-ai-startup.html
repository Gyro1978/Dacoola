US Investment Review Scrutinizes Benchmark's $75M Bet on Chinese AI Startup

The US Treasury Department is examining Benchmark Capital's $75 million investment in Chinese AI startup Manus, according to sources cited by Semafor. The review focuses on compliance with 2023 restrictions on funding Chinese tech firms, despite Manus operating as an AI "wrapper" rather than developing core models.

Manus recently secured funding at a $500 million valuation, with Benchmark leading the round. The startup's Cayman Islands incorporation - a common structure for Chinese firms seeking foreign capital - adds complexity to the US investment review process.

Legal Gray Areas and Geopolitical Tensions

Benchmark's legal team reportedly approved the deal by arguing Manus doesn't create proprietary AI models, instead building applications atop existing systems. They also noted the company's Cayman Islands registration, though this structure is frequently used by Chinese businesses like Alibaba.

The scrutiny comes amid heightened US-China tech tensions. Founders Fund partner Delian Asparouhov publicly criticized the investment, posting on X: "wow, actions have consequences?" This reflects growing skepticism about Western VC funding in Chinese tech sectors affected by export controls.

Regulatory Challenges for AI Investments

The case highlights difficulties in applying investment restrictions to AI startups with ambiguous technical classifications. While Manus doesn't develop base models, its "wrapper" technology could still fall under broad definitions of sensitive AI development.

Neither Benchmark, Manus, nor the Treasury Department provided immediate comments. The review's outcome could set precedents for future cross-border tech investments during ongoing US-China economic tensions.
