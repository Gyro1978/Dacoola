Rising star defense tech startup Mach Industries is raising $100 million, sources say

Mach Industries, a defense tech startup founded by 21-year-old Ethan Thornton, is nearing the close of a $100 million funding round, according to sources familiar with the deal. The financing is co-led by new investor Khosla Ventures and existing backer Bedrock Capital, signaling strong confidence in the company’s trajectory. This latest defense tech funding round will reportedly value Mach Industries at approximately $470 million.

The startup, which has quickly gained attention in the defense sector, has not yet disclosed specific details about how the new capital will be utilized. However, the involvement of high-profile investors like Khosla suggests growing interest in next-generation defense technologies. Bedrock Capital’s continued participation also highlights sustained belief in Mach Industries’ potential.

While the company remains tight-lipped about its product roadmap, the substantial funding round underscores its rapid ascent in the competitive defense tech landscape. With a young founder at the helm and backing from influential venture firms, Mach Industries is positioning itself as a key player in the evolving defense innovation space.
