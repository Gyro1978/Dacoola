Rising star defense tech startup Mach Industries is raising $100 million, sources say

Mach Industries, a defense tech startup founded by 21-year-old Ethan Thornton, is nearing the close of a $100 million funding round, according to sources familiar with the deal. The financing is co-led by new investor Khosla Ventures and existing backer Bedrock Capital, signaling strong confidence in the company’s trajectory. This latest round is expected to value the defense tech firm at approximately $470 million.

The investment highlights the growing interest in defense tech funding, particularly in startups led by young, ambitious founders like Thornton. While specific details about Mach Industries’ technology remain undisclosed, the substantial funding suggests the company is working on high-impact defense solutions. The involvement of Khosla Ventures, a prominent Silicon Valley investor, further underscores the sector’s increasing appeal to venture capital.

Significance

The $100 million raise positions Mach Industries as a notable player in the defense tech space, with a valuation nearing half a billion dollars. This funding could accelerate the company’s R&D efforts and expand its market presence, reflecting broader investor optimism about defense innovation.
