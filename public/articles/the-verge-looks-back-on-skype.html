The Verge looks back on Skype

Skype, once a revolutionary tool for internet-based calls and video chats, is officially shutting down on May 5th after years of decline. Launched in 2003 and acquired by Microsoft in 2011, Skype was a lifeline for long-distance communication before competitors like Zoom and Google Meet took over. Despite its fading relevance, the app’s nostalgic ringtone and role in connecting people globally remain memorable.

Why It Matters

Skype’s closure highlights the rapid evolution of digital communication tools and the challenges of sustaining innovation in a crowded market. Its early dominance paved the way for modern video conferencing, but Microsoft’s shift to Teams signals a strategic pivot. For many, Skype was more than an app—it was a cultural touchstone that bridged distances before smartphones became ubiquitous. Its legacy lives on in today’s seamless, cloud-based communication.
