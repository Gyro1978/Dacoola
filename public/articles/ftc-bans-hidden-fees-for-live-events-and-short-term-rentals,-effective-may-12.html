FTC Bans Hidden Fees for Live Events and Short-Term Rentals, Effective May 12

The U.S. Federal Trade Commission (FTC) has introduced a new "Rule on Unfair or Deceptive Fees," set to take effect on May 12. The regulation targets hidden fees for live events, hotels, and short-term rentals, while also banning misleading pricing tactics like "bait-and-switch" and obscured total costs.

Why It Matters

This move by the FTC represents a significant step toward greater pricing transparency, directly benefiting consumers who have long faced unexpected charges in industries like ticketing and lodging. By cracking down on deceptive practices, the rule could lead to more honest pricing models and reduce frustration for buyers. The regulation may also pressure businesses to streamline their pricing structures, fostering trust in these sectors.
