Over 250 CEOs sign open letter supporting K-12 AI and computer science education

Published on May 05, 2025 by AI News Team   |   View Original Source
<h2>Over 250 CEOs Sign Open Letter Supporting K-12 AI and Computer Science Education</h2> <p>More than 250 CEOs, including Microsoft’s Satya Nadella, Etsy’s Josh Silverman, and Uber’s Dara Khosrowshahi, signed an open letter published in <em>The New York Times</em> advocating for AI and computer science to become "core components" of K-12 education. The letter emphasizes the growing importance of these fields in preparing students for future careers. </p> <h3>Why It Matters</h3> <p>This collective push from top business leaders signals a major shift in how foundational tech skills are perceived in education. By integrating AI and computer science early, schools could better equip students for a workforce increasingly shaped by automation and digital innovation. The CEOs’ involvement also highlights the tech industry’s vested interest in fostering a future talent pipeline—a move that could accelerate policy changes in education systems nationwide.</p>
