Over 250 CEOs Sign Open Letter Supporting K-12 AI and Computer Science Education

More than 250 CEOs, including Microsoft’s Satya Nadella, Etsy’s Josh Silverman, and Uber’s Dara Khosrowshahi, signed an open letter published in The New York Times advocating for AI and computer science to become "core components" of K-12 education. The letter emphasizes the growing importance of these fields in preparing students for future careers.

Why It Matters

This collective push from top business leaders signals a major shift in how foundational tech skills are perceived in education. By integrating AI and computer science early, schools could better equip students for a workforce increasingly shaped by automation and digital innovation. The CEOs’ involvement also highlights the tech industry’s vested interest in fostering a future talent pipeline—a move that could accelerate policy changes in education systems nationwide.
