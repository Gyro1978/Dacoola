Singapore develops Asia’s first AI-based mobile app for shark and ray fin identification to combat illegal wildlife trade

Published on June 08, 2022 by AI News Team   |   View Original Source
<h2>Singapore Develops Asia’s First AI-Based Mobile App for Shark and Ray Fin Identification to Combat Illegal Wildlife Trade</h2> <p>Singapore has pioneered an innovative <strong>AI wildlife conservation app</strong>, marking Asia’s first mobile application designed to identify shark and ray fins. This tool aims to curb the illegal wildlife trade by enabling authorities and conservationists to quickly verify species from confiscated fins. The app leverages artificial intelligence to analyze fin characteristics, streamlining enforcement efforts against poaching and trafficking. </p> <p>The development of this AI-powered solution highlights Singapore’s commitment to leveraging technology for environmental protection. By automating species identification, the app reduces reliance on manual inspections, which can be time-consuming and error-prone. This advancement is particularly crucial for endangered shark and ray species, often targeted in illegal trade due to high demand for their fins. </p> <h3>Significance</h3> <p>The introduction of this AI-based tool represents a significant step forward in wildlife conservation, offering a scalable method to combat illegal trade. With accurate and rapid identification, enforcement agencies can better protect vulnerable marine species, reinforcing global efforts to preserve biodiversity.</p>
