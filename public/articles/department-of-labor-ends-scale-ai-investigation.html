Department of Labor Ends Scale AI Investigation

The U.S. Department of Labor has closed its investigation into Scale AI’s compliance with the Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA), according to a source familiar with the matter. The FLSA regulates worker classification and wage disputes. Scale AI, valued at $13.8 billion, relies on contractors for tasks like data labeling, but former workers have sued over alleged misclassification and unpaid wages.

Why the Investigation Was Dropped

The DOL did not disclose why it ended the Scale AI investigation. However, recent policy shifts suggest a more lenient stance on classifying workers as independent contractors. On May 1, the agency stopped enforcing a Biden-era rule that made contractor classification stricter.

Scale AI’s political connections may also have played a role. CEO Alexandr Wang attended Trump’s inauguration and has advocated for AI leadership under a potential Trump administration. Additionally, former Scale AI executive Michael Kratsios now leads the White House Office of Science and Technology Policy, though his role does not involve DOL oversight.

Legal and Industry Implications

The dropped investigation raises questions about labor protections in the gig economy. Scale AI and its HR partners, Upwork and Hireart, faced scrutiny over worker classification. While the DOL probe is over, lawsuits from former workers alleging wage theft and misclassification remain unresolved.
