Google I/O 2025: AI Takes Center Stage With Gemini, Project Astra, and Android Updates

Google’s flagship developer conference, Google I/O 2025, kicks off on May 20-21 with a heavy focus on AI advancements, including updates to Gemini, Project Astra, and deep integrations across Google’s ecosystem. CEO Sundar Pichai and DeepMind co-founder Demis Hassabis will lead the keynote, outlining Google’s strategy to compete with rivals like OpenAI and Anthropic. The event will also feature major Android announcements, livestreamed talks, and insights into AI-powered search and autonomous driving.

Key Announcements and What to Expect

The Google I/O 2025 keynote begins at 10 a.m. PT on May 20, streaming live from Shoreline Amphitheater in Mountain View. Expect Gemini, Google’s flagship AI model, to dominate discussions, with potential breakthroughs in multimodal AI and real-world applications. Project Astra, DeepMind’s next-gen AI system, may also get a spotlight after last year’s smart glasses teaser.

AI’s role in Google Search will be a major theme, with Elizabeth Reid, head of Search, discussing how generative AI is transforming queries. Other sessions will cover Waymo’s AI navigation, AI agents automating app interactions, and scientific AI applications.

Developer and Consumer Highlights

Following the main keynote, the developer keynote at 1:30 p.m. PT will dive into technical updates for Android, Chrome, and Google Cloud. Later, Demis Hassabis will join a fireside chat at 3:30 p.m. PT, exploring DeepMind’s future and AI’s societal impact.

Separately, The Android Show on May 13 will preview Android updates ahead of Google I/O, offering a dedicated look at mobile innovations.

Frequently Asked Questions

How can I watch Google I/O 2025 live?

The keynote streams at 10 a.m. PT on May 20 via Google’s official channel or embedded players on tech news sites.

What AI projects will be featured?

Gemini AI, Project Astra, and AI-driven updates for Search, Waymo, and apps will be highlighted.

Will Android updates be shown at Google I/O?

Yes, but major Android reveals will debut at "The Android Show" on May 13, followed by deeper dives at I/O.
