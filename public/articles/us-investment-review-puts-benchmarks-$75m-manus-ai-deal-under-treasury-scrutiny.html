US Investment Review Puts Benchmark's $75M Manus AI Deal Under Treasury Scrutiny

The US Treasury Department is reviewing Benchmark's $75 million investment in Chinese AI startup Manus AI, according to unnamed sources cited by Semafor. The probe focuses on whether the deal complies with 2023 restrictions on American investments in Chinese tech companies. Manus AI recently raised funds at a $500 million valuation, with Benchmark leading the round.

While Benchmark's legal team reportedly cleared the investment - arguing Manus operates as an AI "wrapper" rather than a model developer and is incorporated in the Cayman Islands - the review highlights growing regulatory tensions around US-China tech investments. The scrutiny has drawn public criticism from Founders Fund's Delian Asparouhov, who questioned Benchmark's decision on social media.

Legal Gray Areas in AI Investment

The Treasury Department's review centers on whether Manus AI's technical classification and corporate structure circumvent 2023 investment restrictions. Benchmark's lawyers contend the startup doesn't develop foundational AI models, instead building applications atop existing systems - a distinction that could exempt it from certain prohibitions.

The Cayman Islands incorporation, a common approach for Chinese firms seeking foreign capital, adds complexity. While technically offshore, Manus maintains significant operations in China, blurring jurisdictional lines. This structure previously enabled companies like Alibaba to access international investors while complying with Chinese regulations.

Industry Reactions and Implications

The investment review has sparked debate within tech investment circles. Delian Asparouhov's public critique reflects growing concerns about US capital flowing to Chinese AI ventures amid geopolitical tensions. Meanwhile, Benchmark's participation suggests some investors see continued opportunity in China's AI sector, particularly for application-layer companies like Manus.

Regulatory experts note the case could set precedents for how Treasury enforces investment restrictions on AI-adjacent companies. A ruling against Benchmark might chill similar deals, while approval could encourage more "wrapper" investments as workarounds.
