Ray-Ban Smart Glasses Launch in India: AI-Powered Features at ₹29,990

Meta has announced that its Ray-Ban Meta smart glasses will officially go on sale in India starting May 19, priced at ₹29,990 (approximately $353). The glasses, currently available for pre-order on Ray-Ban’s website, will also be stocked in physical stores at launch.

These AI-enabled smart glasses support Meta AI, offering real-time translation, hands-free calls, messaging, and music streaming. While offline translation is available for English, French, Italian, and Spanish, Indian language support is notably absent for now.

Key Features and Market Impact

The Ray-Ban smart glasses stand out with their integration of Meta AI, which can analyze surroundings, translate conversations in real time, and even identify songs via Shazam. In India, users will be able to stream music through popular platforms like Spotify, Amazon Music, and Apple Music directly from the glasses.

Meta has sold over 2 million units globally since the product’s 2023 debut. The India launch marks a strategic expansion into a tech-savvy market, though the lack of local language support could limit broader adoption.

Pros & Cons

Pros
  • **AI-powered live translation** for four major languages, even offline.
  • Seamless integration with **Spotify, Apple Music, and Amazon Music** for on-the-go streaming.
Cons
  • No support for **Indian languages**, a significant drawback for local users.
  • Premium pricing at **₹29,990** may deter budget-conscious buyers.

Frequently Asked Questions

When can I buy Ray-Ban smart glasses in India?

Sales begin on **May 19**, with pre-orders already open on Ray-Ban’s website.

Do the glasses support Indian languages?

No, live translation is currently limited to **English, French, Italian, and Spanish**.

Can I use the glasses without a phone?

Basic features like offline translation work standalone, but calls and messaging require **phone pairing**.
