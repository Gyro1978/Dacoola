Aurora Advances Driverless Truck Expansion with Night Driving and New Routes

Aurora Innovation is accelerating its driverless truck expansion by introducing night operations and extending routes to Houston, El Paso, and Phoenix. The company, which recently launched its first commercial driverless freight service, plans to remove human safety operators in adverse weather conditions by late 2025. Aurora has already completed over 4,000 miles of autonomous freight runs for partners Hirschbach Motor Lines and Uber Freight.

Key Developments in Autonomous Freight

Aurora’s latest shareholder letter outlines ambitious growth plans, including scaling to "tens of trucks" by year-end. The company is collaborating with Paccar and Volvo Trucks to manufacture self-driving trucks at scale, with a shift to a driver-as-a-service model expected by 2027. Despite co-founder Sterling Anderson’s departure, Aurora remains focused on expanding its autonomous freight network.

Financial disclosures reveal $211 million in Q1 operating expenses, including $153 million for R&D. With $1.2 billion in reserves, Aurora anticipates quarterly spending of $175-$185 million for the rest of 2025.
