Generative AI is reshaping South Korea’s webcomics industry

Published on April 22, 2025 by AI News Team   |   View Original Source
<h2>Generative AI is Reshaping South Korea’s Webcomics Industry</h2> <p>Generative AI in comics is making waves in South Korea’s webcomics industry, as legendary cartoonist Lee Hyun-se embraces the technology to immortalize his iconic characters. Known for his seminal series <em>A Daunting Team</em>, Lee has partnered with Jaedam Media to develop an AI model trained on his 46-year archive, enabling the creation of new works in his signature style. His upcoming AI-assisted remake of <em>Karon’s Dawn</em> marks a pivotal moment in blending human creativity with machine-generated art. </p> <p>The rise of AI-powered tools like Onoma AI’s TooToon is accelerating production, cutting development time from months to weeks. While some artists, like Park So-won, resist AI over concerns about artistic identity, others, such as writer-artist Bae Jin-soo, see it as a way to amplify storytelling. The debate reflects broader tensions over copyright and artistic agency, with South Korea’s government still grappling with regulatory frameworks. </p> <h3>Significance</h3> <p>Generative AI is not just streamlining workflows—it’s redefining artistic legacy. For veterans like Lee, AI offers a path to eternalize their creations, while emerging artists navigate its potential and pitfalls. As the industry evolves, the balance between automation and human ingenuity remains central to the future of webcomics.</p>
